## Common SEO Pricing Models and What Each Actually Buys You

### Hourly and Time-and-Materials Pricing

This model bills for time: senior strategist hours, content production, engineering fixes. It is honest and flexible. For B2B tech it works for short audits, migrations, or burst work. The downside is that it rewards scope creep and does not guarantee outcomes. Hourly rates for experienced SEO consultants in 2026 range from **$150** to **$350** per hour. SEO agencies typically charge blended rates that average senior and mid-level time together.

Average SEO cost for a time-and-materials engagement depends entirely on scope. A focused technical audit might run **$10**K to **$25**K. A full migration project billed hourly can exceed **$100**K. The advantage is transparency. The risk is unpredictable spend. For B2B tech companies with defined projects and clear acceptance criteria, hourly pricing can work. For ongoing SEO programs including hourly SEO, it creates friction.

### Monthly Retainers: Capacity or Outcome-Led

A monthly retainer is the dominant choice for sustained SEO investment. It splits into two structures: capacity retainers that provide a set number of hours or roles per month, and outcome retainers that promise specific deliverables like pages published, links acquired, or pipeline milestones.

Capacity SEO retainers provide predictable bandwidth for technical fixes, content operations, and analytics. Outcome retainers tie fees to milestones but often include caveats around timing and attribution. Expect a credible boutique or SEO agency minimum at **$15**K per month for senior teams. Top specialty firms and those including programmatic builds or authority outreach commonly start at **$25**K to **$60**K per month for mid-market B2B tech.

Monthly retainers dominate SEO pricing for good reason. They align incentives around sustained execution rather than one-off projects. For B2B tech companies with 6 to 18 month sales cycles, that continuity matters. The SEO company stays invested in your results because the relationship is ongoing. That alignment produces better strategy decisions than any project-based model.

### Project Pricing: Fixed Scope

Used for migrations, site redesigns, or programmatic SEO builds. Good for discrete businesses, risk-bounded efforts with clear acceptance criteria. Fixed bids avoid hourly surprises but require detailed scoping, which itself costs time. Typical project ranges run **$30**K to **$250**K depending on complexity. A platform migration sits at one end. Building a programmatic taxonomy with templates sits at the other.

Project pricing works when the SEO budget is earmarked for a specific initiative. It does not work as a replacement for ongoing SEO services because search engine optimization compounds over time. Use project pricing for discrete, bounded work. Use retainers for sustained organic growth. If you want the longer version, read our [content marketing pricing](/library/insights/content-marketing-pricing) guide.

### Performance and Revenue Share

Some vendors offer SEO pricing tied to outcomes such as MQLs, revenue, or organic ARR. This aligns incentives but introduces attribution complexity and often requires a long runway for tracking. We prefer hybrid structures where a baseline retainer covers execution, and a smaller success fee is tied to attributable pipeline growth.

Pure revenue share can be risky if attribution or sales cycle length is ambiguous. For B2B tech with complex buying committees and extended deal cycles, we recommend against pure performance models. The SEO cost of misattribution disputes outweighs the potential benefits of shared risk. Hybrid models, where a retainer covers base execution and a bonus is tied to pipeline milestones, provide a balanced approach.

### Productized SEO Packages and Channel-Specific Plans

These are lower-cost, higher-volume offerings: content subscriptions, technical audits, or programmatic page templates. SEO packages and plans typically range from **$3**K to **$12**K per month. They serve tactical gaps but rarely drive strategic gains alone.

Productized SEO services work best when paired with a senior strategy retained by the company — especially when small businesses is involved. If you buy a content subscription without strategic direction, you get volume without intent alignment. The SEO cost per piece might look attractive, but the cost per qualified lead rarely is. Find an SEO package that matches a specific gap in your current program, not one that tries to replace an entire strategy.

## What SEO Pricing Should Include: The Non-Negotiables

Regardless of the pricing model, certain elements must be included in any SEO engagement for B2B tech. If these are missing from a proposal, the SEO cost is higher than it appears because you will pay for them separately or suffer without them.

### Strategy and Prioritization

Every SEO engagement needs a named strategist who owns prioritization. This person decides which keywords to target, which pages to optimize, and how to sequence work for maximum pipeline impact. Strategy is not a one-time deliverable — ecommerce matters here. It is an ongoing function that adapts to competitive shifts, product changes, and performance data. Custom strategy tailored to your business costs more than template playbooks. It is also the only kind that works.

### Technical SEO Infrastructure

Technical SEO is the foundation. Crawlability, indexation, site speed, structured data, and canonical management must be included. SEO agencies that separate technical work from content work create coordination problems. The SEO cost of fixing technical debt after months of content investment is always higher than fixing it first.

### Attribution and Measurement

Any SEO company asking for a monthly retainer should include attribution setup and ongoing measurement in the price. If measurement is an add-on or a separate engagement, the agency is not committed to proving results. SEO pricing that excludes attribution is SEO pricing that hides accountability.

### Reporting and Communication

Weekly leading indicators and monthly business reviews should be standard. Reporting should connect organic activity to pipeline metrics your leadership team cares about. SEO services that report only rankings and traffic are not reporting on business impact. Insist on dashboards that show the relationship between SEO work and revenue outcomes.

## FAQ: SEO Pricing for B2B Tech Companies

### What is the average SEO cost for a B2B tech company?

The average SEO cost for Series A through pre-IPO B2B tech companies ranges from **$15**K to **$60**K per month for ongoing retainer engagements. Project-based work ranges from **$30**K to **$250**K depending on scope. These SEO prices reflect senior-led teams with attribution capabilities. Agencies charging significantly less typically lack the seniority and measurement infrastructure necessary to prove pipeline impact.

### How do SEO agencies typically charge for their services?

SEO agencies typically charge through one of four pricing models: monthly retainers, hourly rates, project fees, or performance-based structures. Monthly retainers are the most common for sustained SEO programs. Hourly models work for audits and burst projects. Performance models exist but require robust attribution. Most B2B tech companies end up on a monthly retainer because the work compounds over time and consistent execution matters more than bursts of activity.

### What should our starting SEO budget be at Series A?

At Series A with **$5**M to **$15**M ARR, plan for a starting SEO budget of **$15**K to **$30**K per month. That covers a senior strategist, tactical content production, technical triage, and attribution setup. Below **$15**K per month, you are unlikely to get senior strategic oversight. The starting investment should produce a prioritized keyword map, first content experiments, and CRM-connected tracking within 30 days.

### Is SEO pricing negotiable?

Yes, but negotiate on scope and milestones rather than rate. Asking an SEO company to discount their monthly fee usually means they cut senior time, which is the highest-value component. Instead, negotiate a 90-day trial period with defined KPIs, milestone-based fee adjustments, factors, and clear exit criteria. That gives you pricing flexibility without compromising quality.

### How do we know if our SEO investment is working?

Measure three key metrics: pipeline attribution from organic sessions, time-to-first-pipeline-impact, and CAC improvement for organic-sourced cohorts. If your SEO agency cannot demonstrate these metrics within 90 days of engagement, the investment is not yielding results. SEO services that generate traffic without pipeline attribution are not delivering ROI, regardless of the SEO cost.

## Conclusion

SEO pricing is less about the number and more about the capabilities behind it: senior strategy, measurable attribution, and execution speed. For B2B tech between Series A and pre-IPO, expect to invest meaningfully. A budget of **$15**K per month is the practical floor for senior coverage. Realistic growth budgets should rise with the complexity of your needs.

Prioritize SEO companies that deliver early results, tie their work to pipeline growth, and clearly define roles. When the pricing model, deliverables, and reporting are transparent, SEO becomes a predictable lever for ARR growth, rather than a recurring expense you dread paying.
