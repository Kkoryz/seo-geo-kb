# The anatomy of a search result | Google Search Central Blog | Google for Developers

Monday, November 26, 2007

When [Matt Cutts](https://www.mattcutts.com/blog),
who heads up Google's webspam team, dropped by our Kirkland offices a little while ago we found
ourselves with a video camera and an hour to spare. The result? We quickly put together a few
videos we hope you'll find useful.

In our first video, Matt talks about the anatomy of a search result, and gives some useful tips on how you can help improve how your site appears in our results pages. This talk covers everything you'll see in a search result, including page title, page description, and sitelinks, and explains those other elements that can appear, such as stock quotes, cached pages links, and more.

If you like the video format (and even if you don't), or have ideas for subjects you'd like
covered in the future, let us know what you think in our
[Webmaster Help Group](https://support.google.com/webmasters/community).
And rest assured, we'll be working to improve the sound quality for our next batch of vids.
