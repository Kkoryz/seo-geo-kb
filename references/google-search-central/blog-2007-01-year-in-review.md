# The Year in Review | Google Search Central Blog | Google for Developers

Friday, January 19, 2007

Welcome to 2007! The [webmaster central](/search) team is very excited about our
plans for this year, but we thought we'd take a moment to reflect on 2006. We had a great year
building communication with you, the webmaster community, and creating tools based on your
feedback. Many on the team were able to come out to conferences and met some of you in person, and
we're looking forward to meeting many more of you in 2007. We've also had great conversations and
gotten valuable feedback in our
[discussion forum](https://support.google.com/webmasters/community),
and we hope this blog has been helpful in providing information to you.

We said goodbye to the
[Sitemaps blog](/search/blog/2006/10/useful-information-you-may-have-missed)
and launched this broader blog in August. And after doing so, our number of unique monthly
visitors more than doubled. Thanks! We got much of our non-Google traffic from other webmaster
community blogs and forums, such as the
[Search Engine Watch blog](https://blog.searchenginewatch.com/),
[Google Blogoscoped](https://blog.outer-court.com/), and
[WebmasterWorld](https://www.webmasterworld.com/). In December,
[seomoz.org](https://www.seomoz.org/) and the new
[Searchengineland.com](https://searchengineland.com/)
were our biggest non-Google referrers. And social networking sites such as
[digg.com](https://digg.com/),
[reddit,com](https://digg.com/),
[del.icio.us](https://del.icio.us/), and
[slashdot.org](https://slashdot.org/) sent
Webmaster Tools many of our visitors, and a blog by somebody named
[Matt Cutts](https://www.mattcutts.com/blog/) sent a lot of
referrers our way as well. And these are the top Google queries that visitors clicked on:

Our most popular post was about the
[Googlebot activity reports and crawl rate control](/search/blog/2006/10/learn-more-about-googlebots-crawl-of)
that we launched in October, followed by details about
[how to authenticate Googlebot](/search/blog/2006/09/how-to-verify-googlebot).
We have only slightly more Firefox users (46.28%) than Internet Explorer users (46.25%). 89% of
you use Windows. After English, our readers most commonly speak French, German, Japanese, and
Spanish. And after the United States, our readers primarily come from the UK, Canada, Germany,
and France.

Here's some of what we did last year.

## January

We expanded into
[Swedish, Danish, Norwegian, and Finnish](/search/blog/2006/01/more-language-support).
You could hear
[Matt on webmaster radio](https://www.mattcutts.com/blog/im-on-webmasterradiofm/).

## February

We lauched
[several new features](/search/blog/2006/02/more-stats-and-analysis-of-robotstxt),
including:

- robots.txt analysis tool
- page with the highest PageRank by month
- common words in your site's content and in anchor text to your site

We met many of you at the
[Google Sitemaps lunch at SES NY.](/search/blog/2006/02/come-by-and-say-hi)
You could hear
[me on webmaster radio](https://example.com/episodes/audio/2006/GK022306.mp3).

## March

We launched
[a few more features](/search/blog/2006/03/more-new-features), including:

- showing the top position of your site for your top queries
- top mobile queries
- download options for Sitemaps data, stats, and errors

## April

We got a whole new look and
[added yet more features,](/search/blog/2006/04/whole-new-look-and-lot-more_26)
such as:

`meta`

tag verification- notification of violations to the webmaster guidelines
- reinclusion request form and spam reporting form
- indexing information (can we crawl your home page? is your site indexed?)

We also added a comprehensive
[webmaster help center](https://support.google.com/webmasters)
and expanded the
[webmaster guidelines](/search/docs/essentials)
from 10 languages to 18.

We met more of you at the
[Google Sitemaps lunch at Boston Pubcon](/search/blog/2006/04/join-us-for-lunch).

Matt talked about the
[new caching proxy](https://www.mattcutts.com/blog/crawl-caching-proxy/).

We talked to many of you at
[SES Toronto](https://www.searchenginestrategies.com/sew/toronto06/index).

## May

Matt introduced you to our new search evangelist,
[Adam Lasnik](https://www.mattcutts.com/blog/better-conversations/).

We spent some time with some of you in our hometown at
[Search Engine Watch Live Seattle](/search/blog/2006/05/live-in-our-hometown) and over
at
[SES London](https://www.searchenginestrategies.com/sew/london06/index).

## June

We launched user surveys, to learn more about how you interact with Webmaster Tools. We
[expanded some of our features](/search/blog/2006/06/get-more-from-latest-release),
such as:

- Increased the number of crawl errors shown to 100% within the last two weeks
- Increased the number of Sitemaps you can submit from 200 to 500
- Expanded query stats so you can see them per property and per country and made them available for subdirectories
- Increased the number of common words in your site and in links to your site from 20 to 75
- Added
`Adsbot-Google`

to the robots.txt analysis tool

[Yahoo! Stores incorporated Sitemaps](/search/blog/2006/06/yahoo-merchants-get-sitemaps)
for their merchants.

## July

We expanded into [Polish](/search/blog/2006/07/support-for-polish).

We began supporting the `<meta name="robots" content="noodpt" />`

tag to allow you
to
[opt out of using Open Directory](/search/blog/2006/07/more-control-over-page-snippets)
titles and descriptions for your site in the search results.

We had a great time talking to many of you about international issues at
[SES Latino in Miami](/search/blog/2006/07/tips-for-non-us-sites).

## August

August was an exciting month for us, as we l
[aunched webmaster central](/search/blog/2006/08/new-name-better-reflects-our)!
As part of that, we renamed Google Sitemaps to Webmaster Tools, expanded our Google Group to
include all types of webmaster topics, and expanded the help content in our webmaster help
center. We also
[launched some new features](/search/blog/2006/08/more-webmaster-tools),
including:

- Preferred domain control
- Site verification management
- Downloads of query stats for all subfolders

In addition, I took over the GoodKarma podcast on webmasterradio for
[two shows](/search/blog/2006/08/listen-in-matt-cutts-and-vanessa-fox)
(one all about
[Buffy the Vampire Slayer!](https://dailysearchcast.com/060831-142957))
and we met even more of you at the
[Google Webmaster Central lunch at SES San Jose](/search/blog/2006/08/chat-with-us-in-person-at-search).

## September

We
[improved reporting of the cache date](/search/blog/2006/09/better-details-about-when-googlebot)
in search results.

We provided a way for you to
[authenticate Googlebot](/search/blog/2006/09/how-to-verify-googlebot). And we started
[updating query stats](/search/blog/2006/09/fresher-query-stats) more often and for a
shorter timeframe.

## October

We launched
[several new features](/search/blog/2006/10/learn-more-about-googlebots-crawl-of),
such as:

- Crawl rate control
- Googlebot activity reports
- Opting in to enhanced image search
- Display of the number of URLs submitted via a Sitemap

And you could hear Matt being interviewed in a
[podcast.](https://www.mattcutts.com/blog/interview-today-with-emarketing-talk-show/)

## November

We launched [sitemaps.org](https://www.sitemaps.org/), for
[joint support of the Sitemaps protocol](/search/blog/2006/11/joint-support-for-sitemap-protocol)
between us, Yahoo!, and Microsoft.

We also started notifying you if we
[flagged your site for badware](/search/blog/2006/11/badware-alerts-for-your-sites)
and if you're an English news publisher included in Google News, we made News Sitemaps available
to you.

Partied with lots of you at "Safe Bets with Google" at
[Pubcon Las Vegas](/search/blog/2006/11/introducing-sitemaps-for-google-news).

We introduced you to our new Sitemaps support engineer,
[Maile Ohye](/search/blog/2006/11/new-third-party-sitemaps-tools), and our first
webmaster trends analyst,
[Jonathan Simon](https://groups.google.com/group/Google_Webmaster_Help-Indexing/browse_thread/thread/614ed0bc5fecbb3e/895656daf4ed1e33).

## Dec

We met even more of you at the webmaster central lunch at
[SES Chicago](/search/blog/2006/12/come-and-see-us-at-search-engine).

Thanks for spending the year with us. We look forward to even more collaboration and communication in the coming year.
