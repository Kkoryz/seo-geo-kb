# BlogHer Tips | Google Search Central Blog | Google for Developers

August 03, 2006

[Yesterday's post](/search/blog/2006/08/back-from-blogher) was about how BlogHer
attendees (and anyone else) can ensure that their web pages get indexed in Google. Here are more
tips:

-
If you want to keep Google from indexing some pages of your site, you can use a robots.txt file,
a simple text file that you place in the root of your site. We provide detailed information
about
[creating a robots.txt file](/search/docs/crawling-indexing/robots/intro). You can also use the[robots.txt analysis tool](https://www.google.com/webmasters/tools/robots-testing-tool)in Google Sitemaps to make sure that your file blocks and allows the pages you intend. -
If Google has indexed content that you don't want to appear in search results, you have two
options:
-
If you want to keep the content on your site, you can block that page with a robots.txt file
and then
[request that we remove the page](/search/docs/crawling-indexing/remove-information)from our index; -
If you remove the content from your site (for instance, you decide that you revealed
information that is too personal and you edit your blog post), you can
[request a cache removal](https://support.google.com/webmasters/answer/7041154).

Good content keeps visitors coming back and compels other sites to link to you. In addition to
blog posts, you can provide othe types of content, such as video. A number of video hosting
sites are available including
[YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/),[Yahoo! Video](https://video.yahoo.com/), and our very own[Google Video](https://video.google.com/). At the conference, some attendees were asking about copyright, and to answer that question, you[retain all rights](https://video.google.com/support/bin/answer.py?answer=26612&topic=8707)to any content you upload to Google Video. -
If you're interested in running ads on your site, take a look at
[AdSense](https://www.google.com/adsense/). The ads are related to what you're talking about that day on your blog—and you can control what ads display.
