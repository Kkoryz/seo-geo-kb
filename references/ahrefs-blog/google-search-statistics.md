# 56 Google Search Statistics to Bookmark for 2024

If you’re curious about the state of Google search in 2024, look no further.

Each year we pick, vet, and categorize a list of up-to-date statistics to give you insights from trusted sources on Google search trends.

- Google has a web index of “about 400 billion documents”. (
[The Capitol Forum](https://thecapitolforum.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/101823-USA-v-Google-PM.pdf)) - Google’s search index is over 100 million gigabytes in size. (
[Google](https://www.google.com/search/howsearchworks/how-search-works/organizing-information/)) - There are an estimated 3.5 billion searches on Google each day. (
[Internet Live Stats](https://www.internetlivestats.com/google-search-statistics/)) - 61.5% of desktop searches and 34.4% of mobile searches result in no clicks. (
[SparkToro](https://sparktoro.com/blog/google-ctr-in-2018-paid-organic-no-click-searches/)) - 15% of all Google searches have never been searched before. (
[Google](https://twitter.com/Google/status/1493681643290300425)) - 94.74% of keywords get 10 monthly searches or fewer. (
[Ahrefs](https://ahrefs.com/blog/long-tail-keywords/)) - The most searched keyword in the US and globally is “YouTube,” and youtube.com gets the most traffic from Google. (
[Ahrefs](https://ahrefs.com/blog/top-google-searches/)) - 96.55% of all pages get zero search traffic from Google. (
[Ahrefs](https://ahrefs.com/blog/search-traffic-study/)) - 50-65% of all number-one spots are dominated by featured snippets. (
[Authority Hacker](https://www.authorityhacker.com/million-serps-analyzed/)) - Reddit is the most popular domain for product review queries. (
[Detailed](https://detailed.com/forum-serps/))

- Google is the most used search engine in the world, with a mobile market share of 95.32% and a desktop market share of 81.95%. (
[Statista](https://www.statista.com/statistics/216573/worldwide-market-share-of-search-engines/)) - Google.com generated 84.2 billion visits a month in 2023. (
[Statista](https://www.statista.com/statistics/268252/web-visitor-traffic-to-googlecom/)) - Google generated $307.4 billion in revenue in 2023. (
[Alphabet Investor Relations](https://abc.xyz/assets/95/eb/9cef90184e09bac553796896c633/2023q4-alphabet-earnings-release.pdf)) - 63.41% of all US web traffic referrals come from Google. (
[SparkToro](https://sparktoro.com/blog/who-sends-traffic-on-the-web-and-how-much-new-research-from-datos-sparktoro/)) - 92.96% of global traffic comes from Google Search, Google Images, and Google Maps. (
[SparkToro](https://sparktoro.com/blog/2018-search-market-share-myths-vs-realities-of-google-bing-amazon-facebook-duckduckgo-more/)) - Only 49% of Gen Z women use Google as their search engine. The rest use TikTok. (
[Search Engine Land](https://searchengineland.com/gen-z-tiktok-google-search-survey-431345))

- 58.67% of all website traffic worldwide comes from mobile phones. (
[Statista](https://www.statista.com/statistics/277125/share-of-website-traffic-coming-from-mobile-devices/)) - 57% of local search queries are submitted using a mobile device or tablet. (
[ReviewTrackers](https://www.reviewtrackers.com/reports/local-search/)) - 51% of smartphone users have discovered a new company or product when conducting a search on their smartphones. (
[Think With Google](https://www.thinkwithgoogle.com/marketing-strategies/search/being-there-micromoments-especially-mobile/)) - 54% of smartphone users search for business hours, and 53% search for directions to local stores. (
[Think With Google](https://www.thinkwithgoogle.com/marketing-strategies/search/how-advertisers-can-extend-their-relevance-with-search-download/)) - 18% of local searches on smartphones lead to a purchase within a day vs. 7% of non-local searches. (
[Think With Google](https://www.thinkwithgoogle.com/marketing-strategies/search/how-advertisers-can-extend-their-relevance-with-search-download/)) - 56% of in-store shoppers used their smartphones to shop or research items while they were in-store. (
[Think With Google](https://www.thinkwithgoogle.com/consumer-insights/consumer-trends/in-store-smartphone-research-statistics/)) - 60% of smartphone users have contacted a business directly using the search results (e.g., “click to call” option). (
[Think With Google](https://www.thinkwithgoogle.com/marketing-strategies/app-and-mobile/smartphone-user-search-behavior-statistics/)) - 63.6% of consumers say they are likely to check reviews on Google before visiting a business location. (
[ReviewTrackers](https://www.reviewtrackers.com/reports/local-search/)) - 88% of consumers would use a business that replies to all of its reviews. (
[BrightLocal](https://www.brightlocal.com/research/local-consumer-review-survey/)) - Customers are 2.7 times more likely to consider a business reputable if they find a complete Business Profile on Google Search and Maps. (
[Google](https://support.google.com/business/answer/10515606?hl=en)) - Customers are 70% more likely to visit and 50% more likely to consider purchasing from businesses with a complete Business Profile. (
[Google](https://support.google.com/business/answer/10515606?hl=en)) - 76% of people who search on their smartphones for something nearby visit a business within a day. (
[Think With Google](https://www.thinkwithgoogle.com/consumer-insights/mobile-search-trends-consumers-to-stores/)) - 28% of searches for something nearby result in a purchase. (
[Think With Google](https://www.thinkwithgoogle.com/consumer-insights/mobile-search-trends-consumers-to-stores/)) - Mobile searches for “store open near me” (such as, “grocery store open near me” have grown by over 250% in the last two years. (
[Think With Google](https://www.thinkwithgoogle.com/marketing-strategies/app-and-mobile/mobile-search-data-for-stores-open-near-me/))

- People use Google Lens for 12 billion visual searches a month. (
[Google](https://blog.google/products/search/generative-ai-search/)) - 50% of online shoppers say images helped them decide what to buy. (
[Think With Google](https://www.thinkwithgoogle.com/marketing-strategies/search/online-shopping-image-statistics/)) - There are an estimated 136 billion indexed images on Google Image Search. (
[Photutorial](https://photutorial.com/photos-statistics/)) - 15.8% of Google SERPs show images. (
[Moz](https://moz.com/mozcast)) - People click on 3D images almost 50% more than static ones. (
[Google](https://www.thinkwithgoogle.com/marketing-strategies/search/search-on-updates/))

- More than 800 million people use Google Discover monthly to stay updated on their interests. (
[Google](https://blog.google/products/search/introducing-google-discover/)) - 46% of Google Discover URLs are news sites, 44% e-commerce, 7% entertainment, and 2% travel. (
[Search Engine Journal](https://www.searchenginejournal.com/google-discover/361142/)) - Even though news sites accounted for under 50% of Google Discover URLs, they received 99% of Discover clicks. (
[Search Engine Journal](https://www.searchenginejournal.com/google-discover/361142/)) - Most Google Discover URLs only receive traffic for three to four days, with most of that traffic occurring one to two days after publishing. (
[Search Engine Journal](https://www.searchenginejournal.com/google-discover/361142/)) - The clickthrough rate (CTR) for Google Discover is 11%. (
[Search Engine Journal](https://www.searchenginejournal.com/google-discover/361142/))

- 91.45% of search volumes in Google Ads Keyword Planner are overestimates. (
[Ahrefs](https://ahrefs.com/blog/gsc-gkp-search-volume-study/)) - For every $1 a business spends on Google Ads, they receive $8 in profit through Google Search and Ads. (
[Google](https://economicimpact.google/methodology)) - Google removed 5.5 billion ads, suspended 12.7 million advertiser accounts, restricted over 6.9 billion ads, and restricted ads from showing up on 2.1 billion publisher pages in 2023. (
[Google](https://blog.google/products/ads-commerce/google-ads-safety-report-2023/)) - The average shopping click-through rate (CTR) across all industries is 0.86% for Google Ads. (
[Wordstream](https://www.wordstream.com/blog/ws/2019/04/01/shopping-ads-benchmarks)) - The average shopping cost per click (CPC) across all industries is $0.66 for Google Ads. (
[Wordstream](https://www.wordstream.com/blog/ws/2019/04/01/shopping-ads-benchmarks)) - The average shopping conversion rate (CVR) across all industries is 1.91% for Google Ads. (
[Wordstream](https://www.wordstream.com/blog/ws/2019/04/01/shopping-ads-benchmarks))

- 58% of consumers ages 25-34 use voice search daily. (
[UpCity](https://upcity.com/experts/consumers-and-voice-search-study/)) - 16% of people use voice search for local “near me” searches. (
[UpCity](https://upcity.com/experts/consumers-and-voice-search-study/)) - 67% of consumers say they’re very likely to use voice search when seeking information. (
[UpCity](https://upcity.com/experts/consumers-and-voice-search-study/)) - Active users of the Google Assistant grew 4X over the past year, as of 2019. (
[Think With Google](https://www.thinkwithgoogle.com/consumer-insights/consumer-trends/google-assistant-user-stats/)) - Google Assistant hit 1 billion app installs. (
[Android Police](https://www.androidpolice.com/google-assistant-app-installs-double-to-1-billion-in-just-7-months/))

- AI-generated answers from SGE were available for 91% of entertainment queries but only 17% of healthcare queries. (
[Statista](https://www.statista.com/statistics/1461813/google-sge-impact-by-industry/)) - The AI-generated answers in Google’s Search Generative Experience (SGE) do not match any links from the top 10 Google organic search results 93.8% of the time. (
[Search Engine Journal](https://searchengineland.com/google-sge-links-organic-search-results-study-436275)) - Google displays a Search Generative element for 86.8% of all search queries. (
[Authoritas](https://www.authoritas.com/blog/research-study-the-impact-of-google-sge-on-brands)) - 62% of generative links came from sources outside the top 10 ranking organic domains. Only 20.1% of generative URLs directly match an organic URL ranking on page one. (
[Authoritas](https://www.authoritas.com/blog/research-study-the-impact-of-google-sge-on-brands)) - 70% of SEOs said that they were worried about the impact of SGE on organic search (
