### 2. Fix Duplicate Content

[Duplicate content](https://www.semrush.com/blog/duplicate-content/) confuses search engines about which version of your pages to show in results, often causing all versions to get deprioritized.

Duplicate content is when identical or very similar content appears on multiple URLs, either within your site or across different websites.

Look for these common duplicate content problems on your site:

- HTTP vs HTTPS versions of the same page
- WWW vs non-WWW URLs
- Multiple URL parameters showing identical content
- Multiple blog posts that cover the same topic with overlapping or near-identical text

The most common culprit is accidental duplication during site setup or migrations—like leaving both HTTP and HTTPS versions accessible, or having both WWW and non-WWW versions live.

When this happens, your rankings strength gets diluted across multiple URLs instead of being consolidated into one strong page, ultimately reducing your visibility and traffic.

Use [Site Audit](https://www.semrush.com/siteaudit/) to quickly find out if you have duplicate content issues across your website. Go to the “**Issues**” tab and search for specific problems. For example, to find if you have “WWW vs. non-WWW” duplicates, search “www” and you'll see if any pages on your site have this common duplication problem.

Or, if you want to find duplicate blog posts, just search for “duplicate” and you’ll see if issues exist.

The tool also provides guidance on how to resolve each issue.

### 3. Use Internal Linking

Internal linking is when you link from one page on your website to another page on the same website.

This practice helps search engines understand the structure of your site and discover all your important pages. It also distributes authority across your website, helping pages rank better in search results.

Strong internal linking can also increase your chances of being featured in AI Overviews, as it signals to Google which pages are most important on your site.

Here's how to build out an effective internal linking structure:

**Link to your most important pages**: Identify the pages that need a ranking boost—key service pages, product pages, or cornerstone content—and prioritize getting internal links pointing to them. This concentrates authority where it matters most for your business.**Use descriptive**: Instead of generic phrases like "click here" or "read more," use anchor text that clearly describes what the linked page is about. For example, "learn about technical SEO audits" tells both readers and search engines what to expect on the other end, which improves relevance signals for that page.**anchor text****Link from high-authority pages**: Pages that already rank well pass more authority through their internal links. If you have a blog post that gets 10,000 monthly visits, adding a contextual link from that post to a newer article can significantly accelerate the newer page's ability to rank.**Keep links contextually relevant**: Only add internal links when they genuinely help the reader find related information. Forced or irrelevant links hurt user experience and can dilute the authority you're trying to pass.

You can find internal linking opportunities using Semrush [Site Audit](https://www.semrush.com/siteaudit/) tool. Navigate to the "Internal Linking" report to see which pages have too few internal links pointing to them and identify opportunities to strengthen your site's link structure.

### 4. Structure Your Content for AI Retrieval

Structuring your content for AI retrieval increases your chances of being cited in in AI-generated results like Google’s AI Overviews and AI Mode.

Being cited matters: Research from Seer Interactive found that sites appearing as sources in AI Overviews saw their organic click-through rate rise from [0.6% to 1.08%](https://www.semrush.com/blog/ai-seo-statistics/). And while overall organic CTR tends to drop when AI Overviews appear on a search results page, sites that earn a citation within the overview capture a disproportionate share of the remaining clicks.

Here’s how to format your content effectively:

**Use descriptive headings**: Break your content into focused sections with H2 or H3 headings. Each heading should clearly indicate what information follows. This makes it obvious to AI what each section is about.**Lead with key information**. Content under those headings should provide a direct answer or summarize the most crucial points upfront. This helps both readers and AI systems quickly identify relevant information.**Write self-sufficient paragraphs**. Since AI tools often extract individual paragraphs, ensure each block of text can be understood without context from surrounding paragraphs. This means making sure each paragraph conveys a complete thought.

These formatting principles apply to all AI-powered search features, including Google’s AI Mode. AI Mode processes queries that are nearly three times longer than traditional searches, and it synthesizes answers from multiple sources. Content that’s clearly structured and self-contained is more likely to be selected as a source.

### 5. Optimize Your Content Around Different Angles of the Main Search Query

Covering multiple angles of a search query increases your chances of ranking and being cited in AI-generated results.

If your content addresses all the angles, you can increase your chances of being used as a reference in AI Overviews.

For example, say you want to rank for "email marketing."

Users searching for this keyword will likely want to know:

- What email marketing is
- What goals they can achieve with email marketing
- How to create effective email campaigns
- What are the advantages and disadvantages of email marketing

As an SEO technique, use this rule of thumb for any keyword before you actually start writing content:

- Google your keyword to see what subtopics are being included in the AI Overview. Make sure you cover those in your own content.
- Additionally, check the "People Also Ask" section for common questions users have about the topic

### 6. Refresh Outdated Content

Refreshing outdated content is one of the fastest ways to regain lost rankings and improve your chances of being cited in AI Overviews. AI systems prioritize fresh, accurate information, so pages with outdated stats, advice, or screenshots are less likely to be referenced.

Here’s how to update your existing pages:

**Identify pages that were published more than a year ago:**Check your CMS or Google Analytics to find older content that may need refreshing, particularly high-performing pages that have seen declining traffic.**Replace outdated information**: Review the content carefully and identify any stats, tools, screenshots, or advice that might be outdated or irrelevant. Replace them with the latest, most helpful information for your audience.**Expand with new subtopics**: Add new subtopics and sections that cover recent developments in the subject matter area**Refresh the publish date**: Whenever you make substantial updates, adjust the publish date or display a “Last Modified” timestamp to signal freshness to Google

### 7. Win Featured Snippets

[Featured snippets](https://www.semrush.com/blog/featured-snippets/) are text excerpts that answer user questions directly and appear near the top of the search engine results page (SERPs).

They can significantly boost your website's visibility and click-through rates.

Finding featured snippet opportunities is pretty easy. Open an incognito browser window and search your target keywords one by one to see which queries currently trigger featured snippets.

The only downside is that manually finding featured snippet opportunities like this will take a lot of time when you have hundreds or thousands of keywords to check.

A better approach is to use an SEO tool like Semrush's [Organic Research](https://www.semrush.com/analytics/organic/). Enter your domain, navigate to the "**Positions**" tab, then apply the "**SERP Features**" filter and select "**Featured snippet**" under "**Domain doesn't rank**."

This shows you all keywords where you rank but don’t own featured snippets.

Once you've identified opportunities, go to the relevant pages and edit their content like this:

- Use a clear, keyword-rich heading that directly addresses the search query
- Match the format of the current featured snippet (paragraph, list, or table). For example, if a query triggers a paragraph snippet, write a concise, complete answer within 40-60 words immediately after your heading. This will make it easier to pull your content into the snippet.

Note that you typically need to rank on the first page (positions 1-10) to have a realistic chance of winning featured snippets.

### 8. Improve E-E-A-T by Working with Experts

Working with subject-matter experts is one of the most effective ways to improve your content’s rankings and AI visibility.

Google evaluates content quality through its E-E-A-T framework—Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness. Content written or reviewed by someone with demonstrable expertise signals quality to both Google’s ranking systems and the AI models that decide which sources to cite. Generic content written without relevant experience tends to get outranked by content that clearly comes from someone who knows the topic firsthand.

Generic content that has been written by someone without relevant expertise or experience is less likely to perform well in Google.

But if the content demonstrates deep knowledge and real-world experience, it's far more likely to rank well (and be featured in AI Overviews).

For example, check [this article](https://www.investopedia.com/articles/basics/12/manage-your-own-money.asp) about money management written by a financial journalist with more than 11 years of experience and reviewed by an experienced investor and financial planner.

Google can detect these expertise signals through author bios, credentials mentioned in the content, and how the advice is framed (generic tips vs. specific, experience-based insights).

Here's how to go about working with experts to create your content:

**Find relevant experts**: Look for people with demonstrable knowledge and experience in your content's topic area. Their involvement adds credibility that both readers and search systems can evaluate.**Get them involved in the content**: Reach out for interviews, direct quotes, or ideally have them write or formally review the piece. Content with expert input tends to include the specific, experience-based insights that generic articles miss, which is exactly what makes it more rankable.**Make their expertise visible**: Add detailed author bios with credentials, and clearly attribute any contributed quotes or insights. This gives Google's quality raters (and AI systems) concrete signals to assess the content's trustworthiness.

### 9. Get Mentioned On Other Websites

Earning mentions on other websites improves your search rankings and increases the likelihood that AI systems will reference your brand in their responses.

Mentions come in two forms:

**Linked mentions**([backlink](https://www.semrush.com/blog/what-are-backlinks/)): Another website references your brand and includes a hyperlink back to your site. These directly improve your domain authority and rankings.**Unlinked mentions**: References to your brand without a hyperlink, like a news article saying "according to Semrush" without linking. AI systems use these signals to determine which brands are authorities on a given topic.

Both matter. Backlinks remain a core ranking factor, but unlinked mentions are increasingly important for AI visibility, where systems like Google's AI Overviews and ChatGPT decide which brands to recommend based on how often they're referenced across the web.

If you're just starting out, earning mentions might seem daunting, but there are realistic first steps you can take.

Start with partnerships in your network: collaborate with complementary businesses on content, co-host webinars, or contribute guest posts to industry blogs. These low-effort tactics can generate your first mentions without needing a PR budget.

Here are some effective ways to earn more mentions:

**Get media to talk about your brand**: Generate a positive buzz about your brand. This can be done by capitalizing on trending cultural moments or launching creative PR stunts. For example, Heinz (a food company) launched a limited-edition “Barbiecue” ketchup to tap into the Barbie movie hype. This earned them a lot of media attention and brand mentions across the web.**Create mention-worthy content**: Create content that other people naturally want to reference—e.g., original studies that uncover new trends, surveys that reveal industry insights, and research that offers contrarian viewpoints.**Leverage speaking opportunities**: Participate in industry conferences, podcasts, and expert panels. When you establish yourself as a knowledgeable voice, mentions often follow naturally.

### 10. Use Schema Markup

[Schema markup](https://www.semrush.com/blog/schema-markup/) is a type of code that gives Google extra context about the content on your page.

In turn, this allows your pages to appear as rich results—those enhanced search listings that show additional information like star ratings, prices, and images.

These visually enhanced results stand out in search and typically see higher click-through rates compared to standard listings.

For e-commerce sites, recipe blogs, review sites, or local businesses, schema is worth prioritizing early since it directly impacts how many people click your results. For informational blogs or simple service pages, it's more optional and nice to have.

Note that schema itself is not a direct ranking factor. Its value comes from improving how your existing rankings perform by attracting more clicks.

There are two main ways to add schema to your site:

**Through your CMS using an SEO plugin**: Plugins like[Yoast](https://wordpress.org/plugins/wordpress-seo/)and[Rank Math](https://wordpress.org/plugins/seo-by-rank-math/)allow you to add schema markup without touching any code. You fill out a form, and the plugin generates and adds the code. You fill out a form, and the plugin generates and adds the code automatically. This is the easiest option for most site owners.**Using a schema generator tool**: Free tools like[Schema.dev's Schema Builder](https://schema.dev)(a Chrome extension) or[Merkle's Schema Markup Generator](https://technicalseo.com/tools/schema-markup-generator/)let you create JSON-LD markup by filling out a form, then copy the generated code into your site's HTML. You can also use Google's[Structured Data Markup Helper](https://www.google.com/webmasters/markup-helper/u/0/), though the tool hasn't been updated in several years and supports fewer schema types than newer alternatives.

Whichever method you use, always validate your schema to make sure it’s implemented correctly.

For individual pages, you can use Google’s [Rich Results Test](https://search.google.com/test/rich-results) tool to see if your implementation is successful.

You can also run a free [SEO check](https://www.semrush.com/siteaudit/) for a quick schema validation. If you're a Semrush user, use Semrush Site Audit tool to scan for schema issues across your entire website.

Navigate to the "Markup" report to see which pages have structured data, what types are implemented, and whether any errors need fixing.

### 11. Improve Core Web Vitals

Improving your [Core Web Vitals](https://www.semrush.com/blog/core-web-vitals/) metrics can lead to a better experience for your website visitors—and better SEO results.

When your pages load quickly and remain stable, users stay longer and engage more with your content.

Google tracks these engagement signals, and sites with better Core Web Vitals performance tend to rank higher because they signal a quality user experience. This also increases their chances of being featured in AI-generated responses.

The three main Core Web Vitals metrics are:

**Largest Contentful Paint (LCP)**: Measures how quickly the main content of your page loads. This should happen within 2.5 seconds or faster.**Interaction to Next Paint (INP)**: Measures how responsive your page is to user interactions like clicks and taps. A page should respond in less than 200 milliseconds for a good experience.**Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS)**: Measures visual stability by tracking unexpected layout changes as your page loads. Keep this below 0.1 to avoid frustrating users.

In our internal study of 50K domains for our [technical SEO checklist](https://www.semrush.com/blog/technical-seo-checklist/), we found that 96% of websites had at least one page that failed the Core Web Vitals assessment test, so there's a good chance your site needs improvement, too.

You can use Google’s [PageSpeed Insights](https://pagespeed.web.dev/) to measure Core Web Vitals performance for individual pages.

For a comprehensive site-wide analysis, use Semrush's [Site Audit](https://www.semrush.com/siteaudit/) tool.

Navigate to the "Core Web Vitals" section to see how your entire website performs across these metrics and get actionable recommendations for the pages that need the most attention.

### 12. Do Programmatic SEO

[Programmatic SEO](https://www.semrush.com/blog/programmatic-seo/) is a technique for creating large numbers of keyword-targeted pages using templates and structured data, driving traffic at scale.

This is an advanced tactic that works best for businesses with unique datasets and development resources—think job boards, real estate sites, or e-commerce platforms with thousands of product variations. If that doesn't describe your situation, the other 11 techniques above will deliver more impact for your time.

Let’s walk through a simple example to see how you might do programmatic SEO. Let’s say you're running a job board. You might have a database of job listings across different locations and industries.

There are lots of people searching for specific combinations like "**software engineer** jobs in **Austin**.”

You can identify programmatic SEO opportunities using Semrush's [Keyword Magic Tool](https://www.semrush.com/analytics/keywordmagic/). Enter a broad keyword related to your business, then look for search query patterns that repeat with different variations.

Once you’ve spotted an opportunity, work with your developer to create programmatic pages. Here are some tips to keep in mind when creating these pages:

**Add unique value beyond raw data**: Don't just display database information. Include helpful context, analysis, or insights specific to each variation. This will help satisfy[search intent](https://www.semrush.com/blog/search-intent/).**Build**: Connect related programmatic pages to help users discover similar variations and strengthen your site's structure**internal links****Avoid thin content**: Each programmatic page should offer substantial value—not just a slightly modified version of the same template with minimal differences.
