# How to Conduct an AI Visibility Audit with Semrush One

An AI visibility audit reveals whether or not your brand is recommended in relevant AI-generated answers about your category.

It answers questions like:

- When someone asks ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google AI about our category, does our brand show up?
- What topics is our brand getting recommended on, and where are we missing?
- Is our current website health and content strategy set up to help us succeed in AI search?

This guide shows you how to use Semrush to answer these questions with an AI visibility audit. You'll measure where the brand appears across AI platforms, which content gets cited, and where topical gaps exist.

**Don't have Semrush yet?** Try our [free AI visibility checker](https://www.semrush.com/free-tools/ai-search-visibility-checker/) to get started without a commitment.

## What to Include in an AI Visibility Audit

Focus your audit on these six signals:

**Overall Visibility & Share of Voice**– How often your brand appears vs. competitors across AI platforms**Brand Mentions**– Frequency your brand name shows up in AI-generated answers**Website Citations**– Which specific pages AI pulls from when forming responses**Topical & Sentiment Gaps**– Topics where competitors are framed more favorably or clearly**Content Opportunities**– Questions AI already answers, but without mentioning your brand**Technical Readiness**– Whether AI crawlers can access and interpret your content

These metrics establish clear benchmarks and point toward high-priority next steps

### Step 1: Establish Your Baseline for AI Visibility

Start by measuring [how your brand appears in AI answers](https://www.semrush.com/ai-seo/overview/) using **Visibility Overview** in the Semrush AI Visibility toolkit.

This dashboard shows mention counts, cited pages, and distribution across AI platforms (ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Perplexity, Gemini).

**Take note: **Write down your brand’s AI Visibility score, mentions, and cited pages. These high-level metrics can be used to benchmark against competitors to see if AI systems reference your brand name or cite your content as a source more than competitors.

For Allbirds, we see 13.5K mentions and 1.1K cited pages.

This baseline now can be compared against other competing brands to see if you have a visibility problem worth solving.

### Step 2: Identify Your Citation-Worthy Content

Next, check which pages AI systems actually cite when generating answers. Navigate to the **"Cited Pages"** section in Visibility Overview.

This shows which URLs from the domain are referenced in AI responses and how many prompts from the Semrush database each page currently gets cited for.

Educational and comparison pages earn more citations than product or category pages.

For Allbirds, their homepage is cited most (90 prompts), but specific how-to content (like travel shoe guides, walking vs. running common questions, and shoe care) also get cited often.

Expand a page to see the prompts and whether the brand name is "Mentioned" or "Missed" within those answers that cite the page.

-
Cited and mentioned: this is a visibility win - Cited but not mentioned: this could be an opportunity to update the page and get mentioned for this prompt (as long as other brands are getting mentioned)

#### Compare AI Cited Pages to SEO Top Pages

You can use the [Top Pages report](https://www.semrush.com/analytics/toppages/) to compare this to the pages that attract the most SEO traffic. Notice in the case of Allbirds, the organic traffic is mostly driven by collection and category pages.

These pages already rank well in traditional search. Now [optimize them for AI retrieval](https://www.semrush.com/blog/how-to-optimize-content-for-ai-search-engines/)—add clear structure, use question-and-answer formats, and concise summaries that AI systems can extract and cite.

### Step 3: Find Topics and Prompts Where Your Brand Is Missing

Topic [gaps](https://www.semrush.com/blog/find-ai-visibility-gaps-with-semrush/) reveal where AI recommends your competitors and omits your brand in a response.

In Visibility Overview, scroll to **"Topics & Sources"** and select **"Topic Opportunities."**

Each topic shows where AI-generated answers exist and attract demand. Expanding a topic reveals the specific prompts producing those answers.

For Allbirds, high-volume topics like "Running Shoes" and "Diabetic and Neuropathy Shoes" show 3 competitors mentioned—but not Allbirds. Each topic can be expanded to reveal the specific prompts generating those answers.

**Action:** Take note of the missed prompts to identify questions that your existing content doesn't answer, then create content that addresses those questions clearly and directly.

### Step 4. Analyze Brand Sentiment and the AI Narrative

Unlike search results, AI doesn’t rank brands by their URL. It can, however, describe a brand as an option with pros and cons alongside other options.

Use the ** Brand Performance** reports to review sentiment across prompts and see which brands are framed positively, neutrally, or negatively.

In this snapshot, Allbirds is usually framed favorably (88%) and holds the largest share of voice (14%) in AI answers compared to the competition.

You can navigate to the Key Sentiment Drivers table in the [Perception Report](https://www.semrush.com/ai-seo/perception/) to review the brand strength factors and areas for improvement—perfect for including in your audit.

The rest of the widgets in the Brand Performance section go deeper into exploring the factors behind a brand’s positive sentiment and areas for improvement in AI answers.

So be sure to explore the rest of those reports, or generate a PDF from this tool in one click.

### Step 5. Identify Influential Off-Site Sources Shaping AI Answers

AI-generated answers often cite third-party UGC platforms like Reddit, YouTube, Quora, LinkedIn, and Medium. Back in the [Visibility Overview](https://www.semrush.com/ai-seo/overview/), check **"Cited Sources"** to see which external domains are most frequently cited across your core topics.

The goal is to assess how much influence these platforms have in your niche. If domains like YouTube and Reddit appear repeatedly, they’re shaping how AI systems explain the category and which brands are referenced.

You can use this data to determine if its worthwhile to invest it growing your brand on YouTube or managing your reputation on Reddit.

### Step 6: Check Site-Level Issues That Affect AI Access

Run a [free SEO check](https://www.semrush.com/siteaudit/) for a quick scan.

For a deeper, site-wide audit covering issues like the ones below, use the Semrush Site Audit tool.

Common issues affecting AI visibility:

**Weak internal context:**Links without descriptive anchor text reduce how clearly pages are framed for AI systems**Structurally isolated pages:**Pages with only one incoming internal link are less likely to be referenced consistently**Missing AI guidance file:**No llms.txt doesn't block access, but removes a clear signal about how AI should interpret site content

These issues don't prevent AI crawling, but they explain why citations come from a smaller set of clearer pages. Fixing them makes more pages easier for AI systems to use as sources.

## What to Do After Your AI Visibility Audit

Once you have an audit, you can decide on what to do next. The roadmap for improving AI visibility should be unique for each business and situation, but here’s a suggested starting point.

**Immediate (Week 1): Fix Technical Issues That Limit AI Access**

Resolve problems affecting whether AI systems can access, interpret, or attribute your content. These fixes have site-wide impact and set the foundation for everything that follows.

**Near Term (Weeks 2–4): Improve Citation Readiness**

Focus on improving content that’s not already cited. Make existing pages clearer, more complete, and easier for AI systems to reuse. After a month, you can re-run your audit to see how much progress has been made.

**Mid Term (Months 2–3): Expand Topic Coverage**

Address the topical gaps by building new content in those areas where your brand is missing.
