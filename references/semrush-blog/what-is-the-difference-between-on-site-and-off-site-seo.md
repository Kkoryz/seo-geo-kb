# What is the difference between on-site and off-site SEO?

The difference between on-site and off-site SEO is that on-site SEO is about improving your website's search engine visibility by optimizing things on your webpages (such as content, the user experience, and technical details), while off-site SEO refers to efforts outside your site that build trust and authority (such as earning links from other websites).

On-site SEO (also known as [on-page SEO](https://www.semrush.com/blog/on-page-seo/)) covers everything you can control about your own webpages, such as load speed, keyword use, internal links, and content structure. By refining on-page elements, you create a strong foundation that helps search engines understand your content and rank your pages higher. A simple example might be optimizing your title tags (HTML titles that can show in search results) and adding clear headings to make your site easier for both visitors and search engines to navigate.

Off-site SEO (also called [off-page SEO](https://www.semrush.com/blog/off-page-seo/)) involves actions taken outside your site, such as earning backlinks from trustworthy websites and getting mentions on social media. These off-page efforts boost your authority and often take longer than on-page efforts because they depend on building relationships and producing content that others want to share. For example, you might write a guest blog post on a relevant industry site so you can drive traffic and show search engines you have credible sources linking back to you.

If you’re trying to decide which area of SEO to focus on first, start with on-site SEO because it can have a more immediate impact on both your search engine visibility and your visitors. Off-site SEO is also important, but it usually takes longer because it involves building trust. You can work on this over time by creating high-quality content that naturally attracts links, forming partnerships, and sharing your expertise on social platforms.

Both on-site and off-site SEO are important for long-term success. It may take time to see results, so you should keep updating your site and building connections across the web. If you’re a small business owner, solo entrepreneur, or are thinking about hiring an agency, start by strengthening your site and then expand your reach over time to maintain steady growth in search visibility.
