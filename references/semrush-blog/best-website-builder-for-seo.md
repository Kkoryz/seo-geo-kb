# 10 Best Website Builders for SEO

There are many website builders to choose from. Each has its own features and functions.

However, when it comes to search engine optimization, not all website builders are equal.

A lot of SEO comes down to things a website builder **can’t** do. Although using one can make launching and maintaining your website faster, whether it’s SEO-friendly or not often comes down to **how** you use it.

That’s why it is important to choose a website builder that has the right functionality (and limits restrictions) for your project.

We've put together a list of the 10 best website builders for SEO to help you make an informed decision.

These tools make it simple to create designs that look good and make your site search engine-friendly.

Here are some of the top website builders to consider:

## What to Look for in an SEO Website Builder

Here's a quick checklist of what you should look out for when choosing an SEO-friendly website builder:

**Mobile responsiveness**: Look for a website builder that ensures your website is mobile-friendly. Ideally, one that automatically optimizes your design for mobile devices.**Page load speed**: Find a website builder that prioritizes fast-loading pages. This builder should use techniques such as caching,[content delivery networks (CDNs)](https://www.semrush.com/blog/content-delivery-networks/), and optimized code to enhance performance.**Customization of on-page options**: A good website builder should provide enough flexibility to customize SEO elements, such as meta tags, headings, and URLs. It should also automate SEO tasks, like redirects.**Technical SEO-friendly**: Check if the website builder allows you to set up and manage essential technical SEO elements easily. Such as[canonical tags](https://www.semrush.com/blog/canonical-url-guide/),[robots.txt files](https://www.semrush.com/blog/beginners-guide-robots-txt/), and redirects. So search engines can correctly crawl and index your site.

Run a free [SEO check](https://www.semrush.com/siteaudit/) to quickly scan for any on-page or technical SEO issues.

For a deeper, site-wide analysis with scheduled audits, use the Semrush Site Audit tool.

To get started, simply head over to the Site Audit tool, enter your domain, and click “**Start Audit**.”

Next, customize your crawl settings.

You can limit the number of pages you crawl, decide whether your site is crawled for mobile or desktop, and allow or disallow specific URLs.

You can also schedule automatic daily, weekly, or monthly audits to ensure your website health stays in top shape.

When you’re happy with your settings, hit “**Start Site Audit**.”

From here, the tool will crawl your site and look for any SEO issues you should be aware of. You will receive an email letting you know when it’s done.

Next, you will be taken to the audit overview. This will give you a score for your overall site health and a list of top issues.

If you head over to the “**Issues**” tab, the tool will tell you which issues it found on your site. It will detail what each issue is, how much of a priority it needs to be, and how to fix it.

From here, you can work your way through fixing the issues. You can even export each issue to your task management software by using the “**Send to…**” button.
