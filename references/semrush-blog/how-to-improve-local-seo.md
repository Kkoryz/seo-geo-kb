# How to Improve Local SEO: 11 Best Practices

Local SEO is the process of optimizing your online presence to help your business appear in search engines when nearby users search for relevant products or services.

Implementing local SEO tactics can help to increase foot and web traffic, manage your online reputation, and compete with nearby businesses.

Plus, ranking for local keywords can drive business results. Because users conducting local searches are typically ready to buy a product or use a service.

Like those looking for a landscaping company or a home cleaning service.

## 11 Tips to Improve Local SEO

Follow these best practices to boost your [local SEO](https://www.semrush.com/blog/what-is-local-seo/):

### 1. Optimize Your Google Business Profile

[Google Business Profile](https://www.semrush.com/blog/google-my-business/) is a free business listing from Google, and optimizing it helps Google understand your business and show it for relevant search terms.

An optimized Google Business Profile can help you appear in the [local pack](https://www.semrush.com/blog/google-local-pack/). This is where Google displays the most relevant local businesses for that search term, along with a map showing their locations.

Once you set up your Google Business Profile, optimize it with Semrush’s [GBP Optimization](https://www.semrush.com/gbp-optimization/) tool.

Enter your business information to see lots of actionable tips to improve your listing and boost your online presence.

### 2. Leverage Directories and Local Citations

Your business is more likely to appear in local search results if its information appears in many trustworthy sources.

These online mentions are known as citations in [local business listings](https://www.semrush.com/blog/local-business-listing/) and other directories. Your business’s name, address, and phone number (NAP) information should be consistent across those sources.

Semrush’s [Listing Management](https://www.semrush.com/listings-management/) tool helps you build local citations. And ensures they display consistent NAP information.

In the tool overview, you’ll see information about your existing listings. And any problems to fix.

You can also see the number of customer reviews, your average star rating, and tips to improve your reviews and ratings.

All businesses should go after general citation sites like Yelp, Tripadvisor, and Foursquare. Because they’re widely recognized. And see lots of website traffic, which means more potential customers will see your listings.

But also consider niche-specific citations. For example, restaurants would be wise to include DoorDash.

### 3. Track and Manage Your Online Reviews

Good reviews positively influence local SEO because they’re among the main factors search engines consider for local rankings.

In this example, the bowling alleys with the highest number of positive reviews top of the results.

Reviews also help build trust with customers. Especially when you make an effort to respond to them.

The [Review Management](https://www.semrush.com/review-management/) tool helps by scanning over 70 directories.

Enter your business name in the search bar and select your business from the drop-down list.

Scroll down to “Manage reputation & track competitors” to see your total number of reviews and average rating.

Click “**Manage reviews now**” to sign up for a plan that will give you access to all of Semrush Local.

Then, go to the “**Reviews**” tab to see all your reviews from different directories.

Click to open reviews in the directory where they occurred. Or respond to Google reviews directly within the tool.

Either way, work to respond to all reviews. To show customers you care.

That’s true even for negative reviews. In fact, a good response to a negative review can positively impact your business image.

Here are some quick tips for responding to negative reviews:

- Respond promptly
- Offer to remedy the situation, such as issuing a refund
- State your commitment to improving your product or service

Finally, encourage your customers to leave reviews. And make the process easy for them by providing clear instructions and direct links to where they can provide reviews.

### 4. Add Local Business Schema to Your Website

[Schema markup](https://www.semrush.com/blog/schema-markup/) is a type of structured data added to webpages to help search engines understand your website and make you eligible for [rich results](https://www.semrush.com/blog/rich-snippets/)—unique search results that stand out from standard results.

[Local business schema](https://www.semrush.com/local/blog/local-business-schema/) is used to convey essential information about local businesses. Including location, operating hours, and the type of business.

[Schema.org](https://schema.org/docs/gs.html) provides a standard vocabulary you can use to define your business type and the key information about it.

You don’t have to be an expert coder to implement local schema markup. Because tools like Google’s [Structured Data Markup Helper](https://www.google.com/webmasters/markup-helper) can help you easily generate basic local business schema.

Here’s how:

Choose “Local Businesses” and enter your URL. Then, click “**Start Tagging**.”

On the left side, highlight the section you want to mark up. And choose the appropriate schema element from the menu.

When you’re done, click “**Create HTML**” in the top right corner.

In this example, we’re tagging the restaurant’s operating hours.

You can then copy and paste the HTML markup into the <head> tag of the appropriate page.

Finally, make sure to follow [Google’s structured data guidelines](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/sd-policies). Violating these guidelines can make you ineligible for appearing in rich results.

### 5. Target Local Keywords

Focusing on [local keywords](https://www.semrush.com/blog/local-keyword-research/) means your content will be relevant to audiences in your area, which can increase the chance that Google will rank your content in local searches.

Local keywords are specific search terms people in your area use to search for products, services, or information.

Semrush [Keyword Magic Tool](https://www.semrush.com/analytics/keywordmagic/) can help you find local keywords to target.

Open the tool, enter a seed keyword (a broad search term that’s relevant to your business) and location. To get personalized recommendations, enter your domain in the “AI–powered feature” bar.

Then, click “**Search**.”

You’ll get a list of related keywords with helpful data about each term’s [search intent](https://www.semrush.com/blog/search-intent/), [search volume](https://www.semrush.com/blog/keyword-search-volume/), and [keyword difficulty](https://www.semrush.com/blog/keyword-difficulty/) score. These metrics guide your content marketing and SEO strategy.

The Personal Keyword Difficulty (PKD %) column shows how difficult it will be for your specific domain to rank in the top 10 results for a given keyword.

To find local keywords, click the “**Include keywords**” drop-down menu, select the circle next to “Any keywords,” and add type in relevant locations.

Then, click “**Apply**.”

You’ll now see a list of keywords specific to your desired locations.

Use this information to choose the relevant local keywords to create content for.

### 6. Create Location-Specific Landing Pages

Creating a dedicated landing page for each business location helps Google understand that each location is a distinct entity and lets you make a focused SEO effort for each location.

On each of these landing pages, include location-specific keywords in the main heading, subheadings, body content, and meta tags.

### 8. Prioritize Local Backlinks

[Backlinks](https://www.semrush.com/blog/what-are-backlinks/) are links from other websites to yours and are an important Google ranking factor.

Links from authoritative websites to yours show Google that your content is valuable and trustworthy. Like votes of confidence.

For local SEO, focus on links from locally-relevant websites. For example, from other local businesses or publications.

Publishing high-quality content can naturally earn you backlinks.

Or you can [build links](https://www.semrush.com/blog/link-building/) by contacting relevant site owners directly and asking for backlinks where it makes sense.

When doing [link building outreach](https://www.semrush.com/blog/link-building-outreach/):

- Create personalized emails tailored to each prospect
- Work to build a relationship with the prospect before asking for backlinks. Like commenting on their content or following them on social media.
- Demonstrate how linking to your website can benefit their business or audience
- Keep a record of your outreach efforts to help you stay organized.

### 9. Optimize for Mobile

Optimize your site for mobile search because most local searches happen on smartphones.

Plus, Google uses mobile-first indexing. Which means it prioritizes the mobile version of your website for indexing and ranking.

Optimizing for mobile means:

- Ensuring you’re using a responsive website that automatically adapts to mobile devices’ smaller screens
- Using font sizes that can be read easily on a mobile device
- Using short paragraphs
- Using a clean and intuitive navigation menu
- Including clear links and buttons that are easy to click

### 10. Participate in the Local Community

Being active in your local community supports your SEO efforts.

This is because:

- Making an impression locally can lead to positive online reviews
- Having a strong, in-person presence locally could encourage local backlinks to your website
- Getting to know your local community helps you understand their needs and interests. And could inspire relevant content or even new products or services.
