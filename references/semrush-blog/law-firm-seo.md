# Law Firm SEO: Top Tactics, Average Costs, & What to Avoid

## What Is Law Firm SEO?

Law firm [SEO](https://www.semrush.com/blog/what-is-seo/) is the process of optimizing a legal website to appear in unpaid search results and ultimately put your website in front of people searching for legal services.

Law firm SEO includes:

**Technical SEO**: Improving a website’s performance, crawlability, and indexability: Optimizing for local search results and Google Maps**Local SEO****Content creation**: Publishing helpful content targeting industry topics and keywords: Earning backlinks (links from other sites) to establish credibility**Link building**

In a 2025 iLawyer Marketing [survey](https://www.ilawyermarketing.com/new-study-how-consumers-choose-lawyers-in-2024/), 92% of respondents said they would use Google to hire a lawyer. So, it’s worth investing in search visibility because it can result in more leads for your business.

## Does Law Firm SEO Work?

Yes, law firm SEO works best when you follow [SEO best practices](https://www.semrush.com/blog/seo-best-practices/) and customize the approach to your law firm’s expertise and audience.

Your strategy should reflect:

- The areas of law you practice
- The regions where you’re licensed
- The competitive landscape of local
[search engine results pages](https://www.semrush.com/blog/serp/)(SERPs) and AI responses - The
[American Bar Association’s Model Rules](https://www.americanbar.org/groups/professional_responsibility/publications/model_rules_of_professional_conduct/)for law firm communications

When done effectively, SEO can deliver benefits beyond site traffic. For example, [Ruler Analytics](https://www.ruleranalytics.com/wp-content/uploads/legal-conversion-benchmark-report-ruler-analytics.pdf) found that organic SEO had the second-highest conversion rate (4.28%) among all marketing channels used by legal businesses.

SEO is a strong long-term investment, and we’ll cover the associated costs in this article.

### Optimize for Mobile Devices and Speed

Google uses [mobile-first indexing](https://www.semrush.com/blog/mobile-first-indexiing/), which means it uses the mobile version of sites for displaying in search results. Plus, ensuring your site loads quickly on all devices is good for the overall user experience, which can impact search visibility.

Start with responsive design, which adjusts website layout and elements to fit the user’s device (mobile, desktop, tablet).

For example, this law firm’s mobile and desktop versions may use the same text, buttons, and images. But the layout adapts to give a good [user experience](https://www.semrush.com/blog/ux-and-seo/) on each device.

Beyond design, improve your [mobile SEO](https://www.semrush.com/blog/mobile-seo/) by:

- Writing mobile-friendly content using short paragraphs, clear fonts, ample white space, etc.
- Avoiding intrusive pop-ups
- Compressing
[images](https://www.semrush.com/blog/image-seo/)and using mobile-friendly formats like WebP or AVIF - Using a content delivery network (CDN) to speed up load times

## Build Trust & Authority

The tactics below strengthen your law firm’s credibility through content, backlinks, and schema markup.

### Create Client-Intent Content

Client intent (or [ search intent](https://www.semrush.com/blog/search-intent/)) is the reason someone searches for and engages with your brand. Content that matches the user’s intent improves their experience and increases the chance of conversion.

The four main search intent types are:

**Informational**: The user wants information**Navigational**: The user is looking for a specific site or brand**Commercial**: The user is comparing options before a purchase**Transactional**: The user is ready to make a purchase or hire a service

Use [Keyword Overview](https://www.semrush.com/analytics/keywordoverview/) to identify client intent.

For example, “best lawyers” has commercial intent (the searcher is still in the research phase). Whereas “get a lawyer” has transactional intent (the searcher is ready to hire soon).

Match your content to the intent. For example, Baron & Associates does a great job of meeting informational intent in this article about the difference between a lawyer and an attorney.

Here’s how they’ve matched intent:

- The blog post explains the topic clearly at the top of the page
- An FAQ answers related questions
- No intrusive pop-ups, submission forms, or call-to-action (
[CTA](https://www.semrush.com/blog/what-is-a-call-to-action/)) buttons, which satisfies the user’s research-focused intent

### Build Quality Backlinks

Getting [backlinks](https://www.semrush.com/blog/what-are-backlinks/) from other websites to your site is likely to improve your visibility in search engines and AI tools.

High-quality backlinks signal that your site is trustworthy. And local backlinks also show your prominence in your community.

For example, [Backlinks](https://www.semrush.com/analytics/backlinks/) shows that this top-ranking New York City law firm has 90,000 backlinks.

And many of that firm’s backlinks come from regional and local publications, such as the New York Post and NYU School of Law.

Some law firm SEO providers may encourage you to buy backlinks without telling you where the links are sourced. Avoid this. Low-quality or spammy links can harm your site’s credibility and raise ethical concerns.

To earn backlinks properly:

- Sponsor or participate in local events
- Offer legal insights to journalists and writers in your field
- Partner with other businesses
- Create helpful guides or resources that other websites want to reference (and link to)

The tone should be friendly, helpful, and credible—without sounding overly self-promotional. Make it suitable for sending to reporters covering legal, business, or general news topics.”*

## Law Firm SEO Costs and Return on Investment (ROI) in 2025

The [ROI of SEO](https://www.semrush.com/blog/seo-roi/) depends on two factors: your investment cost and the revenue SEO generates for your firm.

Here’s the formula for ROI: **SEO ROI = ((return - investment)/ investment) x 100**

In 2025, [a study from Backlinko](https://backlinko.com/seo-pricing) found that SEO costs $1,000 to $2,5000 per month.

Factors that affect SEO pricing include:

**Practice area**: Highly competitive fields, like personal injury, may require larger budgets**Location**: Areas with competing law firms often have higher costs**Services provided**:[Advanced SEO strategies](https://www.semrush.com/blog/advanced-seo/)take more time and resources, increasing costs**Type of provider:**Agencies usually charge more than freelancers, and highly experienced providers may charge premium rates

SEO is often more cost-effective than other marketing channels, like [PPC](https://www.semrush.com/blog/what-is-ppc/), and can deliver strong returns. For example, [PageOne Power](https://firstpagesage.com/reports/law-firm-seo-statistics/) found that over four years, legal clients saw a three-year ROI between 423% and 642%, depending on the practice area.

## Staying Compliant: ABA Rules for Law Firm SEO

The American Bar Association (ABA) allows lawyers to promote their services in [search engines](https://www.semrush.com/blog/search-engine-list/).

However, [ABA Model Rule 7.2](https://www.americanbar.org/groups/professional_responsibility/publications/model_rules_of_professional_conduct/rule_7_2_advertising/) sets ethical standards for all online marketing. Do, use this checklist to ensure your SEO work remains compliant:

- Use your firm’s official name, location, address, and phone number everywhere it appears online
- Clearly list the jurisdictions where you’re licensed to practice
- Make sure
[meta tags](https://www.semrush.com/blog/meta-tag/)are accurate and not misleading - Target local keywords only in areas where you have a physical office
- Avoid keywords unrelated to your practice areas or jurisdiction
- Never write or buy fake reviews. Flag any you believe are fake.
- Avoid unsubstantiated claims (e.g., using “best” without evidence)
- Don’t get backlinks from low-quality or spammy sites, especially those with inaccurate content
- When building links, don’t offer financial incentives for partnerships

## The Red Flag Detector: 8 Questions to Ask a Law Firm SEO Provider

If you [choose an SEO company](https://www.semrush.com/blog/how-to-choose-an-seo-company/) to run your search visibility work, make sure their methods follow both Google guidelines and ABA rules.

Customer reviews, [case studies](https://www.semrush.com/blog/how-to-write-a-case-study/), and awards can help assess credibility. But asking direct questions will reveal whether they follow best practices.

If their answers don’t align with the rules above, consider looking elsewhere.

**Keyword targeting**: How do you decide which keywords make the most sense for my firm?**Location targeting**: How do you determine which areas I should focus on in search?**Directory listings**: Where will my firm’s information be listed online?**Content creation**: Who will be writing the content, and how do they make sure the legal details are correct?**Backlink strategy**: How do you usually get other sites to link to your clients?**AI in content**: Do you use AI in the process, and how do you make sure the final content is accurate and still fits my voice?**Review generational**: How do you help clients get more online reviews?**Overall approach**: What’s your process for making sure everything we do follows industry and legal guidelines?
