# Making AI Your Team’s New Creative Superpower

In a world obsessed with speed, automation, and scale, creativity still wins. But how do we stay creative when our timelines are shrinking, our teams are overwhelmed, and AI tools seem to be everywhere?

Mirjam Schuele, SVP of Marketing at Karl Lagerfeld, offers a refreshing perspective: AI isn’t killing creativity, but redefining it.

Drawing from her experience working alongside iconic designer Karl Lagerfeld for nearly a decade, Mirjam shares a deeply human framework for embracing AI as a co-creator, not a crutch.

For enterprise marketing leaders navigating change-fatigue and innovation pressure, this session provides clarity, compassion, and a practical playbook.

## Watch the interview

Are you navigating AI adoption within a marketing team? Or just trying to reignite your team’s creativity? In either case, Mirjam’s approach offers both empathy and action.

## Why creativity at scale is an enterprise dilemma

It’s a shared reality that many teams face:

- Endless to-do lists, tight deadlines, fragmented tools.
- Pressure to create more content across more formats—without more resources.
- A constant tension between quality, speed, and originality.

In this environment, creativity often feels like a luxury. But it doesn’t have to. AI can help us reclaim creative headspace, when used with purpose.

## 3 ways that AI supercharges creativity

**1. Idea generation at scale**

It’s not about replacing thinking, it's about accelerating it. Forget blank pages. AI helps marketers:

- Brainstorm faster.
- Break creative ruts.
- Remix existing ideas into new formats.

**2. Content drafting and refinement**

AI can support writers and content professionals to:

- Generate initial drafts to kickstart writing.
- Polish and repurpose content for new formats.
- Translate tone and language to fit global markets.

This gives marketers more time to focus on voice, strategy, and story.

**3. Time reallocation**

By offloading repetitive, low-impact tasks like formatting, versioning, and initial copywriting, AI lets creative talent spend more time on what actually moves the brand forward.

This isn’t automation for efficiency’s sake. It’s automation for impact.

## How to move from overwhelmed to empowered

What happened to Mirjam’s team when she first introduced AI to them? Nothing. Not resistance. Not apathy. Just a sense of overwhelmedness.

Without a structured approach to its use, AI can often seem like a nice to have option, or just a lever to pull when time is tight.

The solution:

- Create a safe “test and learn” space.
- Define clear use cases, not vague aspirations.
- Build a culture of curiosity, where experimentation is encouraged and failure is part of the learning loop.

In Mirjam’s case, this shift unlocked participation and tangible creative gains.

## Why AI doesn’t replace creativity, but amplifies it

Mirjam reframes the narrative. AI isn’t stealing jobs. It’s:

- Removing bottlenecks.
- Freeing up mental bandwidth.
- Helping teams do more of what they do best.

For leaders, AI brings clarity through data synthesis, decision acceleration, and insight generation.

For creatives, it’s a force multiplier. Helping to push boundaries, explore new formats, and bring bold ideas to life faster.

## Key takeaways

- AI isn’t a shortcut, it’s a strategic tool for amplifying creativity at scale.
- Use AI for brainstorming, drafting, and automation but keep the human spark in storytelling.
- Adoption isn’t about tools, it’s about culture. Start with small use cases and build momentum.
- The best teams don’t use AI passively. They co-create with it.
