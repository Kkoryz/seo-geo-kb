## Foundational SEO Knowledge

**Estimated learning time***: 2-3 months to learn the basics*

The fastest way to build your SEO chops is by starting your own site. It doesn’t need to be perfect—think of it as your SEO playground.

You’ll be able to practice all of the foundational SEO skills below without worrying about making mistakes on someone else’s site. Plus, it’s proof of your skills when you're ready to pitch yourself.

### 1. Keyword Research

**Impact level:*** Very high ***Learning difficulty:*** Moderate *

Keyword research is the process of identifying search terms people use to find information, products, or services online.

This helps you understand your target audience's search behavior, identify valuable search queries, and determine which keywords to target in your content to increase organic traffic.

To conduct effective keyword research, understanding key metrics is essential:

: The goal behind a query (informational, transactional, commercial, or navigational)**Search intent**: How competitive a keyword is affects how difficult it is to rank**Keyword difficulty**: The number of searches for a keyword over a period**Search volume**: More specific, lower-volume queries that typically bring in targeted traffic**Long-tail keywords**

#### How to Develop This Skill

[Keyword research tools](https://www.semrush.com/blog/keyword-research-tools/) like Semrush’s [Keyword Magic Tool](https://www.semrush.com/analytics/keywordmagic/) help you identify and filter keywords based on the above metrics (and more).

Start by exploring keyword suggestions. Then filter by metrics like search volume, keyword difficulty, and search intent to focus on the most strategic opportunities.

This basic SEO knowledge will help you analyze and select keywords that will drive relevant, actionable traffic to your site.

**Further reading**: [Keyword Research for SEO: What It Is & How to Do It](https://www.semrush.com/blog/keyword-research/)

### 2. Link Building

**Impact level:** Very high **Learning difficulty:** Moderate

Link building is the process of acquiring backlinks from other websites to improve your site's authority, traffic, and search engine rankings.

Quality matters more than quantity—focus on links from relevant, high-quality sites. A single, well-placed link from an authoritative source tends to have more impact than multiple low-quality links.

#### How to Develop This Skill

- Create shareable, valuable content that naturally attracts links
- Find unlinked brand mentions and request a link
- Guest blog on respected sites in your industry

[Link building tools](https://www.semrush.com/blog/link-building-tools/) like Semrush and Ahrefs can help you analyze your site’s backlink profile, compare it with your competitors, and find opportunities for outreach.

The key is earning links that build trust and enhance your site's SEO. Focus on long-term strategies, such as creating high-quality content, that prioritize value and relevance.

### 3. On-Page Optimization

**Impact level:** Very high **Learning difficulty:** Easy to moderate

[On-page optimization](https://www.semrush.com/blog/on-page-seo/) involves refining webpage elements to improve rankings, user experience, and alignment with search intent.

#### How to Develop This Skill

Start by analyzing your pages with Google Search Console (GSC). Open the “Performance Report” to pinpoint pages with low click-through rates (CTR). These are your best opportunities for improvement.

Focus on refining the most impactful on-page elements:

**Title tags and meta descriptions**: Make them concise, relevant, and aligned with search queries. For example, replace*“SEO Tips”*with*“10 Actionable SEO Tips to Boost Rankings Fast.”***Headers**: Ensure your headers logically guide readers and include descriptive, keyword-rich phrasing. Replace vague headings like*“Step 1”*with*“How to Boost CTR with Better Titles.”*: Link related pages using descriptive anchor text. Swap “Click here” for**Internal links***“Optimize meta descriptions.”***Content**: Prioritize readability—short paragraphs, scannable headers, and visuals make a significant difference

### 4. Crawlability and Indexing

**Impact level:** Very high**Learning difficulty:** Moderate

Crawlability determines how easily search engine bots can access your website, while indexability ensures those pages can be added to a search engine’s index. Both [crawlability and indexability](https://www.semrush.com/blog/what-are-crawlability-and-indexability-of-a-website/) are critical. If your pages aren’t indexed, they won’t appear in search results.

To master these skills, you need to understand two key components:

**XML sitemaps:**Files that list all your important pages to help search engines find and crawl your content. Your sitemap should only include canonical (main) URLs and be kept up to date.**Robots.txt files**: This is a text file that tells search engines which parts of your site they can and cannot access. Configure it to block low-value pages (like admin areas) while allowing access to essential resources like CSS and JavaScript files.

#### How to Develop This Skill

Google Search Console is the most effective way to manage crawlability and indexability.

Start by exploring these tools:

**Page Indexing Report:**Shows which pages are indexed and highlights any crawl errors or exclusions**URL Inspection Tool:**Helps diagnose specific indexing issues, such as noindex tags, blocked resources, or server errors**Robots.txt Report**: Shows whether Google can process your robots.txt files, and any warnings or errors

If you find unindexed pages, investigate the cause in Google Search Console and make necessary corrections—like removing noindex tags. Request reindexing through the URL Inspection Tool to ensure those pages can appear in search results.

### 5. Competitor Analysis

**Impact level:** High **Learning difficulty:** Moderate

[Competitor analysis](https://www.semrush.com/blog/how-to-do-seo-competitive-analysis/) involves researching and evaluating the strategies of competing websites to identify opportunities and weaknesses in your own approach.

It’s about understanding what’s working for your competitors—such as their ranking keywords, backlink strategies, and content performance. Then, use that insight to refine your tactics and gain a competitive edge.

Knowing how to do a solid competitor analysis can help you:

- Find keywords that your competitors rank for, but you don't
- Identify and close any
[content gaps](https://www.semrush.com/blog/content-gap-analysis/) - Discover competitor link sources to expand your backlink profile

In the bigger picture, mastering this skill ensures your resources are focused on high-impact opportunities. By analyzing competitors’ strategies, you can choose to focus on tactics with measurable results.

#### How to Develop This Skill

Practice makes perfect. Use competitor analysis tools like [Semrush’s Keyword Gap](https://www.semrush.com/analytics/keywordgap/) to compare rankings against competitors. It provides detailed insights into your competitors' SEO, content, and marketing strategies.

Focus on identifying keywords where they rank in the top 10, and you don’t rank at all.

### 6. SEO Copywriting

**Impact level:** Very high**Learning difficulty:** Easy to moderate

[SEO copywriting](https://www.semrush.com/blog/seo-copywriting/) is creating content that answers user questions clearly and effectively while encouraging engagement and conversions. It’s about understanding what your audience is looking for and delivering it in a way that’s easy to digest.

#### How to Develop This Skill

To excel at this, focus on writing engaging content that addresses both the user’s intent and the structure that supports SEO. Combine persuasive writing techniques and SEO best practices to create:

**Compelling headlines**: Craft attention-grabbing titles that include target keywords naturally**Strong introductions**: Hook readers immediately while addressing search intent**Clear structure**: Use short paragraphs, bullet points, and subheadings to improve readability**Natural flow**: Incorporate keywords smoothly without sacrificing readability**Effective CTAs**: Guide readers toward desired actions without being pushy

Tools like Hemingway Editor and Grammarly will help you refine readability by checking sentence structure, grammar, and clarity.

SEO platforms like [Clearscope](https://backlinko.com/clearscope-review) and [SurferSEO](https://backlinko.com/surfer-seo) will help you optimize your copy by identifying high-value keywords and suggesting related terms to enhance relevance and search rankings.

**Impact level:** Very high **Learning difficulty:** Moderate

Content marketing goes beyond writing copy. It’s about a strategic approach to creating and distributing free content like blog posts, videos, case studies, and infographics. The goal is to attract, engage, and convert visitors using content.

Key content marketing skills required for SEO include:

Effective content marketing provides value by solving problems or answering questions, positioning your brand as an authority.

For optimization, ensure your content is SEO-friendly with tools like Semrush or Yoast, and prioritize readability with tools like Hemingway. Clear, concise writing and proper use of headings and internal links will help improve user experience and drive results.

### 8. Analytic Reasoning

**Impact level:** Very high **Learning difficulty:** Moderate

As an SEO, you often deal with an immense amount of data. This includes rankings, traffic, click-through rates, bounce rates, user behavior, and more.

Analytic reasoning is what helps you make sense of it all. This is the ability to interpret data, identify patterns, and draw meaningful conclusions that can be used to inform your strategy.

If you can look at data and determine where things are going wrong (or right), you’re already miles ahead. This skill is crucial to deliver tangible ROI for your projects.

#### How to Develop This Skill

Spend lots of time reviewing performance in GA and GSC. When analyzing data, always ask “why” at least three times.

For example:

*Why did organic traffic drop?*(The rankings for three key pages fell.)*Why did the rankings drop?*(Competitors’ content outranked ours.)*Why did their content outrank ours?*(They added updated stats and a better internal linking structure.)

Answering these questions helps you dig deeper and find actionable solutions. It’ll also help you communicate issues and solutions clearly to leadership or clients.

### 9. AI Prompt Engineering

**Impact level:** Medium**Learning difficulty:** Easy to moderate

AI prompt engineering is the skill of crafting precise instructions to get accurate results from AI tools like ChatGPT.

I use my prompt engineering for tasks like:

- Creating content outlines and meta descriptions at scale
- Analyzing top-ranking articles’ headlines
- Generating technical elements like JSON-LD schema markup
- Extracting and analyzing SEO data patterns

#### How to Develop This Skill

Start playing around with various [AI tools](https://www.semrush.com/contentshake/content-marketing-blog/ai-seo-tools/) like ChatGPT, Claude, and Microsoft Copilot.

When creating prompts, it’s important to be specific about the task, format, and context. The clearer the prompt, the better the response.

While it may take some trial and error, experimenting with different phrasing can help refine results and reduce guesswork.

For example, a vague prompt like “Cluster this list of keywords” is unlikely to produce the output you want.

But a detailed prompt like "Group the following keywords into three intent-based clusters: 'buy shoes online,' 'best running shoes,' and 'shoe repair near me’” is much more likely to lead to a high-quality response.

Once you understand how to create effective prompts, you can apply AI to tasks like keyword clustering, content creation, technical SEO, and data analysis. Mastering prompt engineering enhances your ability to use AI in your SEO workflow, which will save you time and improve efficiency.

## Technical SEO Skills

**Estimated learning time***: 6 to 12 months to learn the basics *

Technical SEO involves optimizing a website’s backend to ensure search engines can efficiently crawl, render, and index content.

While not every role requires SEO technical skills, understanding [technical SEO](https://www.semrush.com/blog/technical-seo/) allows you to identify issues, diagnose complex problems, and make strategic recommendations for improving site performance.

These skills help you communicate effectively with developers, troubleshoot issues, and ultimately enhance your ability to drive results.

### 10. HTML

**Impact level:** Medium**Learning difficulty:** Easy to moderate

Hypertext Markup Language or HTML defines the meaning and structure of web content.

While many SEOs rely on content management systems (CMS), understanding HTML gives you valuable hands-on control over crucial technical optimizations.

**Headers**: Ensure proper hierarchy (e.g., H1, H2, H3) to improve content readability and relevance for search engines**Meta tags**: Quickly optimize title tags and meta descriptions to align with target keywords and search intent**Alt text**: Add descriptive alt text to images, improving accessibility and helping your site rank in image searches**Canonical tags**: Use canonical tags to avoid duplicate content issues and consolidate link equity**Hreflang attributes**: Implement hreflang tags to target the correct language and regional audiences**Robots directives**: Use robot directives to control which pages are indexed and crawled, helping manage your site’s SEO and crawl budget

This way, you aren’t always dependent on a CMS or a developer, which means you can move faster and make changes right away.

#### How to Develop This Skill

Take an online course from Codecademy, Coursera, or W3Schools. There are also a lot of free tutorials on YouTube.

Once you have the basics down, use Chrome’s "Inspect Element" tool to analyze and troubleshoot HTML in real time. It’s a quick way to spot missing tags, duplicate elements, or areas for improvement without digging through the backend.

### 11. JavaScript

**Impact level:** High **Learning difficulty:** Difficult

Modern websites often rely on JavaScript (JS) frameworks like React, Angular, and Vue.js to load dynamic content. However, if Google can't render and index that content, it won’t appear in search results and won’t rank.

While Googlebot is generally [able to render JavaScript](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/crawling-indexing/javascript/javascript-seo-basics), it’s not always perfect—it can be resource-intensive, slow down indexing, and sometimes fail altogether.

As SEO consultant Nick LeRoy says in his [#SEOForLunch](https://www.seoforlunch.com/) newsletter:

"Not everyone wants to be a technical SEO. However, I would argue that understanding if/how Google renders JS is a critical skill. Get this wrong, and all your other efforts could be for nothing."


#### How to Develop This Skill

To learn JavaScript quickly, check out this helpful guide: [The Beginner’s JavaScript Cheat Sheet](https://www.semrush.com/blog/javascript-cheat-sheet/). Online courses and YouTube will also be great resources for you.

Start exploring this skill further with the URL Inspection tool in Google Search Console. It allows you to view a page the way the Googlebot sees it.

Then, review details like the loaded resources, JavaScript console output, and rendered DOM. It will also display other key information that could help you find the source of the problem.

If there are issues, you may need to implement server-side rendering (SSR) or prerendering techniques, which serve fully rendered HTML to search engines, ensuring your content is visible and indexable.

### 12. XPath

**Impact level:** Medium**Learning difficulty:** Moderate

XPath is a powerful tool for locating and extracting specific elements from HTML or XML documents. Building your skills with this tool will help you streamline audits and automate the extraction of key data such as meta tags, anchor text, and structured data.

XPath is particularly valuable for SEO tasks like extracting structured data (e.g., schema markup or product information), identifying missing or incomplete meta tags (like title tags or descriptions), and analyzing anchor text to optimize internal linking.

#### How to Develop This Skill

First, you’ll want to have a foundational knowledge of HTML and JavaScript. Once you’ve nailed those skills, read an online XPath guide or watch a YouTube tutorial.

To effectively practice XPath, focus on applying what you’ve learned. Start with basic queries like //meta[@name='description']to target specific meta tags. Use tools like Screaming Frog to test these queries, extract website data, and identify patterns.

Take it a step further by auditing websites for common SEO issues, such as missing meta descriptions or broken internal links. Consistent practice in these areas will help solidify your skills and build confidence in applying them.

### 13. Site Speed Optimization

**Impact level:** Medium**Learning difficulty:** Easy to moderate

Site speed optimization ensures your site loads quickly. You do this by compressing images, minimizing code, and using browser caching.

#### How to Develop This Skill

Tools like Google’s [PageSpeed Insights](https://pagespeed.web.dev/) and GTMetrix will show you exactly where you’re losing speed. It might be from oversized images, unused CSS, or a sluggish server.

WebPageTest allows you to diagnose precise performance issues, such as Time to First Byte (TTFB)—the server response time—and render-blocking scripts (JavaScript or CSS files that delay page rendering until fully loaded).

These insights help you avoid generic fixes and target the root causes of slow page loads. Combine WebPageTest with Google PageSpeed Insights for actionable, data-backed recommendations that improve your site's speed effectively.

### 14. Schema Markup

**Impact level:** High**Learning difficulty:** Moderate

Schema is a type of structured data that helps search engines understand your page's content more fully.

By implementing [schema markup](https://www.semrush.com/blog/schema-markup/), you can tell Google exactly what information is on your page—such as a product review, event details, recipe, FAQ, or more.

One key benefit of schema is making your content eligible to appear in rich results. Rich snippets enhance your listing with extra information, such as star ratings, images, pricing, or event dates, making it more engaging and likely to attract clicks.

#### How to Develop This Skill

Start by exploring free guides and tutorials that break down schema implementation step by step. SEO WordPress plugins like Yoast and Rank Math can simplify the process by automating schema setup for common use cases.

That said, learning how to add schema manually is a high-value skill that sets you apart as an SEO professional.

Test your schema with [Google’s Rich Results Test](https://search.google.com/test/rich-results) to ensure it’s valid and eligible for enhanced [SERP features](https://www.semrush.com/blog/serp-features-guide/).

For generic schema validation, use the [Schema Markup Validator](https://validator.schema.org/) to test all types of schema.org markup without Google-specific validation.

### 16. Internal Linking

**Impact level:** High impact**Learning difficulty:** Moderate

Internal linking is the practice of linking one page on your site to another page on your site. It helps distribute authority across your site, improves crawlability, and enhances the user experience. Strategically done, it supports topic clusters and cornerstone content, which are vital for boosting SEO.

#### How to Develop This Skill

Effective internal linking doesn’t require technical expertise but demands a strategic approach.

Focus on:

: Use relevant and descriptive anchor text to improve context and relevance for both users and search engines**Anchor text****Linking structure**: Use tools like Screaming Frog to visualize your internal linking structure, identify orphan pages, and ensure links are distributed efficiently across your site**Prioritize high-authority pages**: Link from high-authority pages to orphaned or lower-authority pages to pass on link equity and boost their visibility

### 17. Excel or Google Sheets

**Impact level:** High **Learning difficulty:** Moderate

Proficiency in Excel and Google Sheets is crucial for managing and analyzing SEO data. These tools help you organize large datasets, track key metrics, and make data-driven decisions efficiently.

#### How to Develop This Skill

Take an online course from Udemy, LinkedIn Learning, or Coursera. Then, practice, practice, practice.

**Pivot tables**: Summarize keyword performance, traffic, or backlink data quickly**Formulas**: Use functions like VLOOKUP to match and retrieve data from different tables or datasets and SUMIF to calculate metrics like CTR**Filtering and sorting**: Organize data by important criteria, such as keyword difficulty or traffic sources**Dashboards**: Create simple, visual dashboards to track key SEO metrics, making it easier to report results

### 22. Prioritization

**Impact level**: Medium impact**Learning difficulty**: Easy to moderate

Effective SEO prioritization means focusing on tasks that deliver the greatest impact, like resolving indexing issues or improving site speed, while deprioritizing less critical tasks, such as tweaking meta descriptions.

#### How to Develop This Skill

You’ll naturally develop this skill as you get more SEO experience.

To allocate resources effectively, adopt frameworks like the Eisenhower Matrix to categorize tasks by urgency and importance. This ensures you focus on high-priority efforts that drive results first.

For example, prioritize urgent tasks that affect site performance and long-term efforts like link building while saving less critical changes for later.
