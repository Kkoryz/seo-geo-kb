# AI visibility: What it is and how to grow yours in 2026

As more people turn to AI platforms like ChatGPT and Google AI Mode to find products, compare options, and make decisions, appearing in traditional Google results is no longer enough.

Your brand can rank in position 1 on Google and still be invisible in AI-generated answers.

In this guide, you'll learn what AI visibility is, how to measure it, and what you can do to grow your brand’s AI search presence.

## What is AI visibility?

AI visibility is how often your brand is mentioned, cited, or recommended in AI-generated responses across platforms like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Mode.

For example, when I asked ChatGPT about the best platforms for building an online store, the response mentioned Shopify, WooCommerce, Wix, BigCommerce, and Squarespace, with a description and comparison of each.

Because a single answer in a single platform doesn't show how strong a brand's overall presence is in AI search, I ran Shopify through Semrush's [AI Visibility Toolkit](https://www.semrush.com/ai-seo/). I saw that it had a pretty good overall presence but still some room to improve.

## Why is AI visibility important?

AI visibility is important because AI search is becoming one of the primary ways people look for solutions, discover products, and make decisions.

Google remains the dominant discovery channel, but AI platforms are growing fast. ChatGPT alone now has more than [900 million weekly users](https://openai.com/index/scaling-ai-for-everyone/).

The visitors coming from AI platforms also tend to be valuable. In fact, [Semrush research](https://www.semrush.com/blog/ai-search-seo-traffic-study/) estimates that AI search visitors convert 4.4x better than traditional organic search visitors. Likely because AI visitors arrive having already researched their options.

And that value is only expected to grow. AI search channels are projected to drive as much business value as traditional search by 2027 — and surpass it soon after.

## How does AI visibility optimization differ from traditional SEO?

Traditional SEO focuses on showing higher in traditional search engine results, while AI visibility optimization focuses on appearing more prominently in AI-generated answers.

Think of AI visibility optimization as an extension of SEO. Core signals like relevance to a query, authority, and page experience are still important.

But AI systems don't merely show results as links. They assemble comprehensive answers from multiple sources. And how they choose and use content from those sources depends on factors like how your brand is mentioned elsewhere on the web, how recent your content is, and how easy it is to understand your content.

The important thing to know is that ranking well in traditional search results doesn’t guarantee you’ll be cited in AI-generated answers.

## How to measure your AI visibility

You can measure your AI visibility by deciding on your platforms and prompts, understanding which metrics to track, and then gathering the data manually or with a dedicated tool.

Here's how to get started.

### Step 1: Pick your platforms and prompts

Start by identifying which AI platforms your audience uses most and what questions they're likely to ask on those platforms.

The simplest way to identify platforms is to ask directly. If you can, talk to customers, prospects, or your sales team about which AI tools your audience turns to regularly.

You can also get a sense of what platforms your audience uses by reviewing your traffic sources. If you already see referral traffic from a specific AI platform in your analytics, that's a clear signal to prioritize it.

Once you know which platforms to focus on, build your prompt list. Think about the questions your audience asks at different stages of the buying journey.

**Research**: "What is X," "how does X work," "best practices for X"**Comparison**: "X vs Y," "best tools for [activity related to your niche]," "alternatives to Y"**Evaluation**: "Is X worth it," "X pricing," "pros and cons of X," "X reviews"

Think about highly specific phrases that are relevant to your brand. Because they're closer to how people actually phrase questions in AI platforms.

And check the People Also Ask (PAA) box in Google for the keywords you're already targeting for your SEO efforts. The PAA section surfaces questions real users are asking, and they’re often similar to AI prompts your audience is using.

Community forums like Reddit are another place to see what your audience is asking.

Find subreddits relevant to your brand, search them for mentions of your primary SEO keywords, and sort the results by "Top." The questions that rise to the top reflect questions your audience is asking.

### Step 2: Know what metrics to track

For each prompt, track these metrics:

**Mentions**: Whether your brand appears in the AI-generated answer at all**Citations**: Whether that answer includes a link to your site**Position**: Where in the response your brand appears relative to other brands mentioned**Sentiment**: Whether your brand is being described positively, neutrally, or negatively

You can also track other metrics like share of voice and visibility trend over time with a dedicated tool.

### Step 3: Track your AI visibility

Once you’ve defined the platforms, prompts, and metrics you’ll use to measure your AI visibility, use one of the following methods for tracking:

#### Conduct a manual AI visibility analysis

Use your prompt list to enter each prompt into your chosen AI platforms one by one.

For each prompt, log:

- Whether your brand appears in the response
- Whether the response includes a link to your site
- Where your brand appears relative to other brands mentioned
- How your brand is described (positive, neutral, or negative)

Keep your results in a simple spreadsheet with columns for the prompt, platform, date, and each of the above data points. This gives you a baseline you can compare against over time.

## How to improve brand visibility in AI search

You can improve your [brand visibility](https://www.semrush.com/blog/brand-visibility-what-is-it-how-can-i-increase-it/) in AI search by making your brand easier for AI systems to find, understand, and reference using these tips:

### Target the questions your audience asks AI

Content built around the specific questions your audience asks AI platforms is more likely to appear in AI-generated answers.

To find those questions:

- Check customer support tickets and sales call notes for recurring questions
- Search Reddit, Quora, and other online communities in your niche for questions people ask about your topic
- Look at the People Also Ask sections that appear in Google for your main topics. These often reflect the same questions people ask AI platforms.

For a more comprehensive and accurate list, use Semrush's [Prompt Research](https://www.semrush.com/ai-seo/prompt-research/) tool. It groups related prompts by topic, so you can create one piece of content that targets several prompts at once rather than writing a separate page for each.

Once you have your list, create content that directly addresses those topics.

### Publish original content

AI systems have a tendency to surface content that includes unique information over generic pages covering the same topics in the same ways.

Consider including first-party data, case studies, and your own expert insights in the content you create. At Semrush, we do this consistently by publishing original studies.

Here are some practical ways to create original content:

- Publish research or survey findings from your own customer base
- Write case studies based on real results from your customers
- Share expert perspectives from people inside your organization
- Document experiments or tests your team has run and what you learned
- Break down industry reports, studies, or trends and share your own analysis and perspective

And keep your original content fresh.Nearly 90% of pages crawled by AI bots were published within the last three years, according to a [Seer Interactive](https://www.seerinteractive.com/insights/study-ai-brand-visibility-and-content-recency) study.

### Structure your content for AI extraction

Structuring your content clearly makes it easier for AI systems to find, understand, and cite specific parts of your page.

Start by formatting your content in clearly defined sections, each focused on one idea. This is called [chunking](https://www.semrush.com/blog/content-chunking/).

AI systems don’t read a page from top to bottom the way a human does. They pull specific sections that answer a query, so each section should make sense on its own.

Also, use a heading hierarchy with [H1](https://www.semrush.com/blog/h1-tag/) leading into H2s, H2s leading into H3s, etc., without skipping levels. This helps AI systems understand the structure and relationships between sections on your page.

Here are a few other ways to make content easier to extract:

- Use question-based headings that mirror how your audience phrases their queries
- Start each body section with a direct answer to the heading's question (or implied question)
- Keep important information visible on the page. Don’t hide it inside expandable sections or tabs that require a click to open.
- Include specific stats, named experts, and concrete data points AI systems can reference

Independent reviews, news articles, and features in industry publications all give AI systems more places to discover and learn about your brand. So, aim to get coverage in those places.

- Publish original research, tools, and other valuable resources that gives other publishers something specific to reference and cite
- Contribute guest articles or expert quotes to publications your audience already reads
- Participate in industry events, podcasts, and expert roundups to build your brand's presence across credible sources

### Show up across multiple formats

Taking a multimodal approach — publishing across written, video, audio, and visual formats — gives AI systems more ways to find and reference your brand.

- Turn a blog post into a YouTube video or a short video series
- Repurpose a research study into a LinkedIn carousel or an infographic
- Use a key finding as the basis for a podcast episode or expert interview
- Break down a long-form guide into a series of social posts or short clips

Consider prioritizing platforms AI systems already pull from frequently. LinkedIn, YouTube, and Reddit are some of the most-cited domains in AI-generated answers.

### Shape perception

Consistent brand messaging across your owned properties helps AI systems accurately understand and describe who you are.

If your about page, product pages, and social profiles use different language to describe what you do, AI systems may piece together an inconsistent or inaccurate picture of your brand.

Start by auditing:

- Your homepage, about page, and product or service pages
- Your social media profiles and bios
- Any directory listings or third-party profiles

Also, manage how your brand is represented elsewhere by responding to reviews on platforms like G2 and Trustpilot and engaging in relevant threads on Reddit and Quora. These responses become part of the content that AI systems read.

For example, the co-founder of Tally (a form builder) regularly engages with people on Reddit. In her responses, Marie Martens consistently reinforces that Tally offers unlimited forms and submissions for free — the same core message that appears across all of Tally's owned properties.
