# 22 Best Content Creation Tools for Creators

Google’s AI Overviews and evolving quality expectations have changed what it takes to create content that actually performs.

Publishing more content (or relying entirely on generative AI) isn’t enough. Creators now need tools that help them research smarter, work more efficiently, and optimize content for both human readers and AI-driven search experiences.

Today’s strongest creator workflows combine tools for writing, design, video, audio, and distribution—many of them powered by AI. Together, these tools support the full content life cycle.

In this guide, we’ve rounded up the **22 best content creation tools for creators in 2026**, based on hands-on testing and real-world use cases.

## Research & SEO Tools

### 1. Keyword Magic Tool

Keyword Magic Tool, the premium version of Semrush’s [free keyword research tool](https://www.semrush.com/analytics/keywordmagic/), is a solution for discovering and organizing search-driven content opportunities at scale.

In our test, the tool generated 223,000+ keyword ideas from a single [seed keyword](https://www.semrush.com/blog/seed-keywords/) almost instantly. From there, we were able to filter the list based on [search volume](https://www.semrush.com/blog/keyword-search-volume/), [keyword difficulty](https://www.semrush.com/blog/keyword-difficulty/), and [search intent](https://www.semrush.com/blog/search-intent/) to surface keywords that align with both traffic potential and ranking feasibility.

What sets Keyword Magic Tool apart is its AI-powered Personal Keyword Difficulty metric, which estimates how hard it would be for your specific site to rank for a keyword. This is especially useful for choosing realistic, intent-aligned keywords.

If you’re planning content meant to compete in search results or appear in AI-generated summaries, Keyword Magic Tool helps you prioritize topics with real visibility potential.

#### What We Like

The Personal Keyword Difficulty score provides fast, actionable insight into which keywords are worth targeting based on your site’s authority.

#### Pricing

A free trial of Semrush’s full SEO Toolkit is available. Paid plans start at $117.33 per month.

### 2. Google Trends

[Google Trends](https://trends.google.com/trends/) is a free tool from Google that lets you explore trending searches worldwide and across different regions.

In our test, it took us two minutes to uncover five trending topics we could cover on our blog, simply by using the “**Trending Now**” tab along with the “**Category**” drop-down filter within Google Trends.

Google Trends is a great tool if you’re focused on creating reactive content. Those looking to cover evergreen topics are better off using something like Keyword Magic Tool.

#### What We Like

The “Trend breakdown” section makes it easy to understand why a specific search is trending and offers additional search terms you can look into.

#### Pricing

Google Trends is available for free.

### 3. Topic Research

[Topic Research](https://www.semrush.com/topic-research/) helps you generate content ideas based on a single seed topic, making it easier to plan content clusters that align with real search demand.

With one click, we generated 400+ related topic ideas, grouped by subthemes and user intent. This makes Topic Research particularly useful for creators building editorial calendars, pillar pages, or long-term content strategies.

The Mind Map view is especially valuable for visualizing how topics connect, helping you spot opportunities to build topical authority. This kind of structured planning is increasingly important for AI-driven search systems that evaluate content depth and relevance across an entire site.

If you want to move beyond one-off blog posts and build a cohesive content ecosystem, Topic Research provides a strong starting point.

#### What We Like

The Mind Map feature makes it easy to see relationships between topics and plan content that supports long-term SEO growth.

#### Pricing

A free trial of the Semrush SEO Toolkit is available. Paid plans start at $117.33 per month.

## Content Writing & Editing Tools

### 4. Semrush Content Toolkit

The Semrush [Content Toolkit](https://www.semrush.com/content/) is an AI-powered suite designed to support a complete search-optimized content workflow—from topic discovery to optimization to publishing.

Using the toolkit, we were able to go from a broad topic idea to a fully structured, search-optimized article in about 10 minutes. The process starts with Topic Finder, where you enter a topic and audience location to generate relevant ideas along with supporting explanations and title suggestions.

From there, you generate an SEO content brief that includes recommended keywords, search intent insights, metadata, and an optimized outline. That brief can then be sent directly to the AI Article Generator to create a draft that aligns with SEO and AI SEO recommendations.

What makes the Content Toolkit especially valuable is its focus on optimization after writing. The built-in Content Optimizer highlights opportunities to improve clarity, relevance, and keyword coverage. To ultimately help your content perform better in both traditional search results and AI-generated answers.

For creators who want one system to handle research, briefing, writing, and optimization, this toolkit removes the need to juggle multiple tools.

#### What We Like

The Content Optimizer makes it easy to apply search-friendly optimizations and improve readability without rewriting entire sections manually.

#### Pricing

The Semrush Content Toolkit costs $60 per month.

### 5. ChatGPT

[ChatGPT](https://chatgpt.com/) can act as your creative content partner, helping you with brainstorming ideas, creating outlines, or generating full drafts.

In our test, we used ChatGPT to create a 2,000-word first draft for an article in five minutes. The caveat: you need to do prior research and design a good prompt in order to get decent results.

ChatGPT is a good option for kickstarting content creation, but it’s not great at generating content that’s instantly publishable.

Any articles you create with ChatGPT usually require extensive fact-checking and multiple revisions to get them to a point where they’re fit for publication.

#### What We Like

Where ChatGPT shines is in revising existing content. Use it to add keywords to an article, adjust style and tone of voice, and shorten long sections or paragraphs without losing meaning.

#### Pricing

A free plan is available. Paid plans start at $20 per month.

### 6. Grammarly

[Grammarly](https://www.grammarly.com/) is an AI-powered writing assistant that provides suggestions to improve your writing.

In our test, Grammarly pointed out a dozen opportunities to improve a recent article. We were then able to implement improvements in a single click thanks to its AI-powered suggestions.

Grammarly is a great option for any team looking to improve the quality of their content and ensure an error-free reading experience for their audience.

#### What We Like

Grammarly makes it easy to ensure all your content adheres to your brand guidelines by allowing you to create a style guide within the tool.

#### Pricing

A free plan is available. Paid plans start at $30 per month.

## Photo-Related Tools

### 7. Lightroom

[Lightroom](https://lightroom.adobe.com/) is a popular photo editing tool that allows creators to edit, manage, and organize their photos.

In our test, we used it to import 20 RAW images, apply a preset, and export web-ready JPEGs in under five minutes.

Lightroom is the tool of choice if you’re looking to do advanced photo editing. With plenty of features and options, it might not be the best choice for beginners, though.

#### What We Like

Lightroom’s cross-device capabilities make it perfect for editing on the go. For example, you can start editing photos on your smartphone, and then continue seamlessly on desktop since changes are synced automatically.

#### Pricing

A free trial is available. The paid plan costs $11.99 per month.

### 8. Photopea

[Photopea](https://www.photopea.com/) is a browser-based photo editor and a free alternative to Lightroom.

We were able to edit and resize a blog post image in under three minutes by using Photopea, and that’s without even having to create an account or log in.

Photopea is a great option for when you need to quickly touch up an image and don’t require advanced tools.

#### What We Like

Since it’s browser-based, Photopea runs smoothly even on older, lower-end laptops and tablets. Which allows you to edit photos from almost any device.

#### Pricing

Photopea is free.

### 9. Unsplash

[Unsplash](https://unsplash.com/) is a free stock photo platform that includes millions of photos you can use in your content creation projects.

We used Unsplash to find a high-quality image for an article and discovered dozens of suitable images in under a minute after using the search feature and drop-down filters.

It’s great for finding royalty-free images to use in blog content or on landing pages. Those looking for premium visuals for use in ad campaigns will likely need to look elsewhere, though.

#### What We Like

Unsplash’s massive image library covers almost every topic out there, making it suitable for a wide variety of projects.

#### Pricing

A free plan is available. The paid plan costs $16 per month.

Canva is a good choice for small teams that need to design branded assets without spending too much time or resources.

#### Pricing

A free plan is available. Paid plans start at $15 per month.

### 11. Remove.bg

[Remove.bg](https://www.remove.bg/) is a background removal tool that automatically detects an image’s background and allows users to download a background-free image which contains only the subject.

Removing a background from an image took less than a minute while using the tool.

Remove.bg is a great option if you’re looking to quickly touch up a profile photo or thumbnail.

#### What We Like

Remove.bg lets you edit thousands of images at once with its Bulk Edit feature, which can save you plenty of time if you regularly manage product catalogs, marketing visuals, or large sets of photos that need consistent background removal.

#### Pricing

A pay-as-you-go plan is available. Subscription plans start at $8.10 per month.

## Audio & Podcasting Tools

### 15. Audacity

[Audacity](https://www.audacityteam.org/) is an audio editing tool that’s used for editing podcasts, recording voiceovers, and adding sound effects to audio tracks.

For our test, we used the Macros feature in Audacity to batch process 10 podcast clips, normalizing audio levels and reducing background noise. The whole process took under five minutes.

Audacity is best for those times when you need to do a quick voiceover or some basic audio cleanup.

#### What We Like

Audacity supports a wide range of plugins, which you can use to expand the software’s core capabilities.

#### Pricing

Audacity is free.

### 16. Alitu

[Alitu](https://alitu.com/) is a podcast editing software that allows you to edit and trim podcast recordings, remove background noise, and more.

We edited a podcast episode with Alitu by using one-click background noise removal, incorporating volume normalization, adding background music, and publishing the final podcast to Apple Podcasts—the entire process took just under 20 minutes.

Alitu is a great choice for users who value speed and automation. But power users might find it a tad too limiting.

#### What We Like

Alitu eliminates the need for using multiple tools to record, edit and publish podcasts, allowing you to do everything from one place.

#### Pricing

A free trial is available. The paid plan costs $38 per month.
