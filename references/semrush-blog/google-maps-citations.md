## What Are Google Maps Citations & Why Do They Matter for Local SEO?

Google Maps citations are mentions of your business’s name, address, and phone number ([NAP](https://www.semrush.com/local/blog/nap-seo/)) across the web that Google uses to verify your information, which can then improve your visibility in map results.

These mentions include:

- Google Business Profile
- Directories
- Review sites
- Social media
- Local newspapers
- Blogs

Citations play an important role in local SEO because they help Google confirm the details and location of businesses. When [Google Maps](https://www.semrush.com/local/blog/how-to-boost-your-google-maps-ranking/) finds consistent citations, it’s more likely to trust your business, leading to better visibility.

Each Google Maps citation is a confirmation that users can rely on your information.

If a [roofing company’s](https://www.semrush.com/local/blog/roofing-marketing/) NAP is consistent across the web, Google Maps should happily display it in results. But if there are discrepancies—such as different addresses—this can confuse Google Maps and lower the business’s ranking.

Whether you’re in retail, run a service-based business, or a restaurant, managing citations helps attract local customers. And failing to do so can see your business literally fall off the map.

Fortunately, it’s possible to take control of your Google Maps citations and avoid losing rankings.
