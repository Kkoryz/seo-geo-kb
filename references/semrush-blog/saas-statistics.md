# 60 SaaS Statistics Related to Growth, Challenges, and More

If you’re in the software-as-a-service (SaaS) space, understanding market dynamics is key

What does the data say about SaaS growth rates? How much do companies typically spend on SaaS solutions? And what factors do brands consider when making SaaS purchases?

Let’s dive into the latest SaaS stats to find out.

## SaaS Industry Statistics Highlights

- The global SaaS market is set to generate nearly $819 billion in 2029
- The U.S. SaaS market is projected to surpass $445 billion by 2029
- Almost 50% of all venture capital investments were made in SaaS startups in 2023
- Microsoft is the SaaS company with the largest market capitalization (the total value of a company's publicly traded shares) in the world at more than $3 trillion
- Artificial intelligence (AI) software is the biggest SaaS sector based on customer count, with 3 billion global customers
- Organizations use 130 SaaS apps on average
- The average B2B SaaS churn rate is -1.59%
- Globally, the SaaS inflation rate is nearly four times higher than the market inflation rate

## SaaS Overall Market and Growth Statistics

### General SaaS Market Stats

The SaaS industry continues to expand rapidly, driven by increasing global demand and advancements in cloud technology.

Here are some key figures that capture the growth and scale of the worldwide SaaS market:

- Global SaaS market revenue is projected to reach $818.8 billion by 2029 (
[Statista](https://www.statista.com/outlook/tmo/public-cloud/software-as-a-service/worldwide#revenue), 2024)

**Image Data Source**: Statista

- The global SaaS market is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate of 13.7% from 2023 to 2030 (
[Grand View Research](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/saas-market-report), 2023) - The North American SaaS market alone was valued at $131.18 billion in 2023 (
[Fortune Business Insights](https://www.fortunebusinessinsights.com/software-as-a-service-saas-market-102222), 2024) - In 2022, the global cloud computing market was valued at $413 billion, and SaaS made up $185 billion of that total (
[Statista](https://www.statista.com/statistics/540525/worldwide-cloud-saas-revenue-share-by-vendor/), 2023)

## SaaS Financial Statistics

### Company Revenue

Revenue and profitability vary widely across SaaS companies, along with funding sources and growth strategies.

These stats give a snapshot of how different types of SaaS companies perform financially:

- Almost 80% of bootstrapped companies are profitable or breaking even. This figure is just 37% for equity-backed companies. (
[SaaS Capital](https://www.saas-capital.com/blog-posts/spending-benchmarks-for-private-b2b-saas-companies/), 2024) - In 2023, bootstrapped companies reported a median growth of 25%. While equity-backed companies reported a median growth of 30%. (
[SaaS Capital](https://www.saas-capital.com/research/private-saas-company-growth-rate-benchmarks/), 2024) - High-growth SaaS companies spend 50% or more of their revenue on sales and marketing (
[McKinsey & Company](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/technology-media-and-telecommunications/our-insights/saas-and-the-rule-of-40-keys-to-the-critical-value-creation-metric), 2021) - The median revenue per employee for private SaaS companies is $125,000 (
[SaaS Capital](https://www.saas-capital.com/blog-posts/revenue-per-employee-benchmarks-for-private-saas-companies/), 2024) - Equity-backed SaaS companies with $1 million to $3 million in annual recurring revenue (ARR) have a median ARR per employee of $73,333. This figure is $108,000 for bootstrapped companies. (
[SaaS Capital](https://www.saas-capital.com/blog-posts/revenue-per-employee-benchmarks-for-private-saas-companies/), 2024)

### User Spending

Businesses of all sizes continue to invest heavily in SaaS solutions, with recent price increases also contributing to the rise in spending.

Here’s a closer look at current spending patterns in the SaaS market:

- Global user spending on SaaS is estimated to reach over $295 billion in 2025. (
[Statista](https://www.statista.com/statistics/505243/worldwide-software-as-a-service-revenue/), 2024)

**Image Data Source**: Statista

- Almost 35% of Businesses spend between $2.4 million and $12 million on SaaS solutions (
[Statista](https://www.statista.com/statistics/1472733/organizations-saas-annual-spend-globally/), 2024) - In 2024, businesses spend around $8,700 per employee on SaaS products. This is up from $7,900 in 2023. (
[Vertice](https://www.vertice.one/l/saas-inflation-index-report), 2024) - About 45% of small- to medium-sized businesses (SMBs) report spending up to $600,000 annually on SaaS. While around 16% spent up to $2.4 million. (
[Statista](https://www.statista.com/statistics/1472790/smb-saas-annual-spend-globally/), 2024) - The cost of SaaS solutions has increased by 33% since 2021 (
[BetterCloud](https://pages.bettercloud.com/rs/719-KZY-706/images/BetterCloud-State-of-SaaSOps-2024.pdf), 2024) - On average, SaaS prices increased by 12% between 2022 and 2023 (
[Vertice](https://www.vertice.one/insights/august-2023), 2023) - Globally, the SaaS inflation rate is almost four times higher than the standard market inflation rate (
[Vertice](https://www.vertice.one/l/saas-inflation-index-report), 2024)

### Pricing Models

SaaS companies use a mix of pricing strategies to meet diverse customer needs and market demands.

Here are some of the key pricing trends shaping SaaS today:

- Of surveyed SaaS companies, 23% have usage-based subscription tiers. And 18% have a largely usage-based model (
[OpenView Venture Partners](https://openviewpartners.com/blog/2023-pricing-data/), 2023) - Around 60% of surveyed SaaS companies make an effort to adapt their pricing to local markets (
[OpenView Venture Partners](https://openviewpartners.com/blog/2023-pricing-data/), 2023) - About one-third of global survey respondents say per-user pricing is their preferred pricing model. Use-based pricing came in second place, with around 30% of votes. (
[Statista](https://www.statista.com/statistics/1452696/saas-product-pricing-model/), 2024) - Only 45% of SaaS companies publish their pricing (
[OpenView Venture Partners](https://openviewpartners.com/blog/saas-pricing-insights/), 2021) - Around 60% of SaaS vendors deliberately mask price increases (
[Vertice](https://www.vertice.one/l/saas-inflation-index-report), 2024)

## SaaS Use and Adoption Statistics

### Use and Purchasing Decisions

The decision-making process for SaaS purchases is influenced by several factors, from security concerns to productivity goals.

Here are some stats to illustrate how organizations choose their SaaS solutions.

- Organizations use 112 SaaS apps on average (
[BetterCloud](https://pages.bettercloud.com/rs/719-KZY-706/images/BetterCloud-State-of-SaaSOps-2024.pdf), 2024) - Of surveyed businesses, 37% say boosting productivity is their main reason for buying new software. Other top reasons include addressing security concerns (30%), expanding product offerings (27%), and targeting new customers (26%). (
[Gartner](https://emt.gartnerweb.com/ngw/globalassets/en/digital-markets/documents/2023-global-software-buying-trends-report.pdf), 2023) - More than 40% of businesses say that security is their top consideration when buying new software (
[Gartner](https://emt.gartnerweb.com/ngw/globalassets/en/digital-markets/documents/2023-global-software-buying-trends-report.pdf), 2023) - More than 80% of software buyers take into account a vendor’s history with software breaches when studying software solutions (
[G2](https://research.g2.com/2024-buyer-behavior-report), 2024) - For 33% of businesses, there are two to three people on the software buying committee (
[Gartner](https://www.gartner.com/en/digital-markets/insights/state-of-software-investment-2024), 2024)

**Image Data Source**: Gartner

- Almost 40% of businesses always involve the finance team in software purchase decisions (
[Gartner](https://www.gartner.com/en/digital-markets/insights/state-of-software-investment-2024), 2024) - Over 50% of organizations scrutinize their SaaS purchasing decisions more than in previous years (
[BetterCloud](https://pages.bettercloud.com/rs/719-KZY-706/images/BetterCloud-State-of-SaaSOps-2024.pdf), 2024) - Around 40% of SaaS licenses are going unused (
[Productiv](https://productiv.com/state-of-saas), 2024) - The average company wastes over $135,000 in unused software licenses (
[BetterCloud](https://pages.bettercloud.com/rs/719-KZY-706/images/BetterCloud-State-of-SaaSOps-2024.pdf), 2024)

### Churn

Minimizing customer churn is a top priority for SaaS companies.

Here are a few insights into the current state of customer retention within the SaaS space:

- In March 2024, the average B2B SaaS churn rate was -1.59%. That’s 12% lower than in March 2023. (
[Paddle](https://www.paddle.com/blog/saas-market-report-march-2024), 2024) - In 2023, 68% of businesses replaced software more frequently than in 2021 (
[Gartner](https://emt.gartnerweb.com/ngw/globalassets/en/digital-markets/documents/2023-global-software-buying-trends-report.pdf), 2023) - Of B2B SaaS companies with knowledge of how their integrations affect churn, 92% report less churn by customers who use integrations (
[Partner Fleet](https://go.partnerfleet.io/2024-state-of-saas-integrations), 2024)
