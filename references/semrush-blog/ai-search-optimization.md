# How to optimize for AI search results in 2026

People ask ChatGPT direct questions, rely on AI Overviews for instant answers, and expect synthesized information from multiple sources.

So optimizing for AI systems determines whether your content gets cited and recommended, or stays invisible.

**Use this guide to optimize for AI search, track your AI visibility, and stay ahead as AI reshapes how content gets discovered.**

For a comprehensive technical deep-dive, check out our [generative engine optimization](https://www.semrush.com/blog/generative-engine-optimization/) guide.

## What is AI search optimization?

AI search optimization is the practice of making your content frequently referenced and prominently featured by AI search systems like ChatGPT, Google's [AI Overviews](https://www.semrush.com/blog/ai-overviews/), and Perplexity.

AI search optimization is also known as generative engine optimization (GEO) or SEO for AI search. This [large language model (LLM) optimization](https://www.semrush.com/blog/llm-optimization/) approach focuses on getting cited more often and positioned higher in AI-generated responses rather than ranking well in organic search engine results.

Success requires understanding how these platforms select sources and making sure your content consistently appears as a preferred citation, ideally as the primary or first-mentioned source.

## How AI search optimization differs from SEO

[SEO](https://www.semrush.com/blog/what-is-seo/) optimizes for search engine rankings. AI search optimization optimizes for mentions and citations.

AI search optimization isn't a replacement for SEO. It's the next layer of the same discipline, applied to AI-powered surfaces alongside search engine results.

The nuanced differences between AI search optimization and SEO can be confusing. They're leaving many marketers wondering whether the methods used to optimize for each are different in practice.

Think of it this way: AI systems now act as intermediaries between users and content. They filter and synthesize information instead of linking to it.

This changes how people search and consume information:

**People ask full questions**. Instead of looking for "email marketing tips," they ask: "What's the best way for B2B companies to increase email open rates?"**AI provides complete answers**. Users get comprehensive responses without needing to click through to multiple sites.**Brand awareness matters more than traffic**. Being cited builds authority even when users don't visit your site.**Multiple sources get combined**. AI pulls information from several sites to create one answer.

All of these differences mean you need to write differently. Focus on being easily referenced and cited, not on being comprehensive.

## How to optimize for AI search

Optimize for AI search by starting with the highest-impact changes below.

### 1. Verify AI systems can access your content

Verify AI systems can access your content, because they won’t be able to cite you if they can’t view your content in the first place.

Check your [robots.txt](https://www.semrush.com/blog/beginners-guide-robots-txt/) file at yoursite.com/robots.txt. Look for blocks against common AI crawlers like:

**OAI-SearchBot, GPTBot, and ChatGPT-User**: OpenAI’s crawlers for ChatGPT**CCBot**: Common Crawl’s crawler, which collects web crawl data for anyone to use for free**ClaudeBot**,**Claude-User**, and**Claude-SearchBot**: Anthropic's crawlers for Claude

If you see Disallow: / for these crawlers, you're blocking AI access.

**Quick action**: Search for your brand name or key topics on ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude. If your content never appears as a source, you may have accessibility issues.

Check that your most important pages aren't blocked by:

- Login walls or paywalls
- JavaScript-only navigation that crawlers can't follow
- Server errors or extremely slow loading times

Also confirm your [canonical tags](https://www.semrush.com/blog/canonical-url-guide/) are present and pointing to the correct pages. Broken or missing canonicals don't block AI crawlers, but they can cause AI systems to cite the wrong version of your content or skip it as duplicate.

### 2. Add specific statistics to your content

Add specific statistics — with sources — to your content because AI systems reference content with specific, sourced data more often than content with vague generalizations.

Start with 2-3 articles that already rank well for your target keywords.

Replace vague statements like 'AI Overviews are appearing in many searches' with concrete data like the **16% figure** from the Semrush AI Overviews study cited above. Use the source name, the date range, and the methodology when you can: "As of December 2025, Google’s AI Overviews appear in about 15% of search results according to [Semrush Sensor](https://www.semrush.com/sensor/). "

Like this:

This single change often produces the fastest AI citation improvements.

**Quick action**: Spend 30 minutes finding and adding 2-3 relevant statistics to your top-performing articles with proper attribution.

### 3. Test your topics on AI platforms

Test your topics on AI platforms to identify opportunities for plugging gaps in your content and improving its AI visibility.

- Ask different AI platforms the same questions your audience would ask about your niche or expertise.
- Note which competitors get cited and what types of sources appear.

If you're consistently missing from results while competitors appear, you've identified a content gap.

Analyze how AI systems phrase their responses. This shows you what structure and phrasing they extract cleanly. Then mirror that structure in your own content.

For example, if AI responses consistently use numbered lists or start with direct definitions, incorporate these elements into your articles.

**Quick action**: Test five questions related to your main topic across Gemini, Perplexity, ChatGPT, and Google's AI Overviews.

Document which sources get cited and analyze what they do differently from you. Then, identify one element from these sources to incorporate into your next piece of content.

### 4. Structure content for direct answers

Structure content for direct answers by ensuring each content section addresses its heading’s topic completely.

AI systems extract chunks of content. As a result, they can more easily understand and cite sections that provide direct answers. This is especially true if the sections make sense without context from other parts of the page.

Use question-based headings like "How to reduce website loading time" rather than vague titles like "Performance tips."

For example, start with "You can reduce website loading time by optimizing images, enabling compression, and choosing faster hosting" before diving deeper.

**Quick action**: Rewrite the headings in one existing article to directly answer your audience’s questions. Then, rewrite each heading’s opening paragraph to provide a complete answer before you expand on details.

### 5. Make your content quotable

Make your content quotable by including expert quotes, case study results, and concrete examples, because these make your content more citation-worthy.

Generic statements like "Our approach works well" are vague and lack credibility. Instead, mention numbers, timeframes, and measurable outcomes.

For example: "When we reduced client page load time from 4.2 to 1.8 seconds, organic traffic increased 43% within two months."

This short case study gives AI systems something concrete to reference when supporting broader claims about site performance.

Beyond making your content quotable, establish yourself as a quotable source.

Add your credentials and experience to your author bio. AI systems look for authority signals when deciding which sources to cite.

**Quick action**: Add one specific statistic, case study result, or named expert quote to your highest-traffic article.

These changes won't transform your AI visibility overnight, but they create the foundation that makes other AI search optimization techniques more effective.

### 6. Keep your content updated

Keep your content updated, because AI search systems prefer citing recent content.

This is the case even for evergreen topics:

Other AI systems also prioritize recency. Metehan Yeşilyurt, Co-Founder and Chief Growth Officer at AEO Vision, posted about this [on LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/metehanyesilyurt_i-found-a-chatgpt-5-configuration-with-some-activity-7363875273815003136-x-iE/):

“ChatGPT prioritizes RECENT over PERFECT. That amazing guide from 2022? It's losing to mediocre content published yesterday.”


**Quick action**: Look for pages that are critical to your business and any pages losing visibility. Use[Google Analytics 4](https://www.semrush.com/blog/google-analytics/) to find pages with diminishing traffic.

Then, optimize these pages’ content for AI search by:

- Incorporating recent statistics from reputable sources
- Modernizing discussions in alignment with recent developments (e.g., updating an article from last year about ChatGPT to discuss ChatGPT’s new models)
- Gathering new and original data and adding relevant insights (e.g., updating a “best tools” roundup article to include newer, more effective tools and your findings from testing them)

### 7. Optimize for query fan-out

Optimize for query fan-out to improve your content’s visibility for a wider range of topics.

Query fan-out is the process of AI systems splitting user prompts into related sub-queries, and then using information relevant to each sub-query to synthesize an answer to the initial prompt.

By optimizing your content for query fan-out, you position it as an information source for multiple related topics, which increases the likelihood of AI systems mentioning and citing your content.

Your content can't just answer users' main questions; it also needs to address the predictable sub-questions AI systems generate.

**Quick action**: Run your target topic through an AI tool and note the sub-topics, entities, and follow-up angles that the tool explores while generating an answer.

Then, check whether your content addresses each of these angles. And plug content gaps accordingly.

Check out our detailed [query fan-out guide](https://www.semrush.com/blog/query-fan-out/) for more information on optimizing for it.

### 8. Optimize differently for each AI platform

Optimize differently for each AI platform, because they retrieve and cite content differently from each other, and optimizing for one platform doesn’t guarantee visibility on others.

For example, we’ve observed that:

**Google AI Overviews**retrieves content from[Google’s search index](https://www.semrush.com/blog/google-index/)to generate answers with curated citation links**Gemini**retrieves content from its training data and Google’s search index to generate answers with inline source citations**ChatGPT**retrieves content from its training data. With web search enabled, it also pulls from web sources via its own crawler (OAI-SearchBot) and Bing's search index, and displays inline source citations.**Claude**retrieves content from its training data. If web search is enabled, Claude can also retrieve content from the[Brave Search](https://brave.com/search/api/)index (Anthropic's web search partner) and display inline source citations.**Perplexity**retrieves content from its own search index to generate answers with inline source citations

Optimize your content according to these characteristics. For example, to get your content cited in Google AI Overviews, ensure that your content is in Google’s index and fulfills [Google’s Search technical requirements](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/essentials/technical).

**Quick action**: Run the same question across different AI platforms. Note where your competitors appear in each platform's answer and where your site isn't cited. Then optimize your content for the platforms where you have less visibility.

### 9. Build entity authority beyond your site

Build entity authority beyond your site, because AI systems evaluate a brand's credibility using **both on-site and off-site signals**: what your site says about your brand, and what the rest of the web says.

Build off-page entity authority by taking steps like:

**Running****digital public relations****campaigns**to earn[brand mentions](https://www.semrush.com/blog/brand-mentions/)and backlinks from relevant industry publications. Use a tool like[Backlink Gap](https://www.semrush.com/analytics/gap/backlinks/)to discover industry publications that have linked to your competitors but not your brand, so you can pitch these publications.**Building brand presence and authority on community forums**like Reddit and Quora by having team members comment on relevant forum posts. In these replies, focus on delivering value by providing helpful answers to users' questions. Avoid promotional content, because some forums prohibit self-promotion and may remove non-compliant content.**Monitoring your brand’s positioning on authoritative platforms**like Wikipedia and Google Business Profile, and working to make your positioning across them accurate and consistent. When these platforms corroborate each other, you help AI systems deliver a brand narrative that aligns with how you want users to perceive your brand.**Designating team members to respond to interview requests**and secure subject matter expert quotes across reputable third-party sites. Where possible, your team members’ responses should include specific examples and data to boost authority.

**Quick action**: Run a web search in ChatGPT or Perplexity for your top three target topics. Then check whether the cited sources mention or link to your brand. If your brand isn't mentioned, pitch those sources for coverage.

### 10. Use schema markup

Use schema markup to help search systems better understand your content and improve your AI visibility.

Schema markup is a way of formatting your page's content to match standardized data structures that search systems understand. Schema comes in many types. For example, Article schema looks like this:

Using schema markup can improve your organic search rankings, which AI systems reference when finding trustworthy content to cite.

Prioritize using these schema markup types:

**Article**: Article schema helps define the content’s author, publication date, and topic, which AI systems reference when evaluating the content’s authoritativeness and trustworthiness**FAQ**: FAQ schema maps questions directly to answers for easier AI extraction**HowTo**: HowTo schema structures content in a step-by-step format that AI systems can interpret and turn into instruction lists more easily**Product**and**Review**: Product and Review schema provides essential ratings information that helps your content appear in AI-generated "best of" recommendations

**Quick action**: Use Google’s [Rich Results Test](https://search.google.com/test/rich-results) to check if your top 3-5 pages contain schema markup. If they don’t, use a tool like the [Structured Data Markup Helper](https://www.google.com/webmasters/markup-helper/u/0/) to add Article, FAQ, or other suitable schema markup to them.

### 11. Avoid common AI search optimization mistakes

Avoid common AI search optimization mistakes that hinder your efforts to improve AI visibility.

These mistakes include:

**Accidentally blocking AI crawlers**: Open your robots.txt file and check the user agents listed in Step 1 (GPTBot, ClaudeBot, OAI-SearchBot, etc.) aren't disallowed**Optimizing for only one AI system**: Different AI systems retrieve and cite content differently, and you’ll need an optimization strategy for each one**Failing to address user intent in your content**: User intent refers to the user’s purpose for conducting the search. AI systems prioritize citing content that meets users’ needs**Publishing thin, AI-generated content**: AI systems prefer citing unique, in-depth, and well-supported content**Letting your content become outdated**: AI systems favor citing up-to-date content**Focusing only on on-page optimization tactics**: AI systems also consider off-page entity authority when citing sources**Treating AI search optimization as a separate discipline from SEO**: The fundamental tactics are the same, with an added focus on earning AI citations on top of improving organic search rankings
