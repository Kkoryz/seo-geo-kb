# Content Gap

Add a top 1% marketer to your team

Module 10

Content Gap is our competitive analysis tool that lets you see keywords which your competitors' websites rank for, but yours doesn’t.

Here are some actionable use cases for it.

Perform content gap analysis when creating new content. This involves finding the keywords your competitors rank for, but you don’t – it’s one of the quickest ways to find hundreds of great keywords that your website should be ranking for

Let’s say that we want to create new content for the Ahrefs blog.

First, open the Competitive Analysis tool and select “keywords”. Then add our blog at the top and a couple of our organic search competitors in the bottom section.

Hit [Show keyword opportunities](https://app.ahrefs.com/competitive-analysis/content-gap?cmp-how-many=1&cmp-must-include=&cmp-position=top10&competitors=moz.com%2F%7Csubdomains%7C%7Csearchenginejournal.com%2F%7Csubdomains&country=us¤tDate=today&keywordRules=&limit=100&offset=0&serpFeatures=&sort=volume&sortDirection=desc&target=ahrefs.com%2F%7Csubdomains&target-position=no-position&volume_type=monthly), and we’ll see what keywords we have in common with Moz and SEJ where at least one of them ranks in the Top 10, but Ahrefs isn’t ranking anywhere in the Top 100.

Not all of them will be a good topic for us to go after. To narrow them down, click on the Competitors positions dropdown and select All competitors to find keywords where both competitors rank, but we don’t.

This brings us down to 202 keywords that we could potentially target, like “pillar page”, advanced seo”, and so on.

This is one of the fastest methods to shortcut your way to position #1 without doing too much link building and on-page optimizations

To find your competitors’ featured snippets:

Click on the More dropdown

Select SERP features

Select Where target doesn’t rank, then the Featured snippet checkbox


And hit Show results. Next, apply a Target filter for positions: from 2-10. We exclude keywords in position #1 on purpose since that’s where SERP snippets are found.

This shows us all the keywords where there’s a chance to rank with a featured snippet.

This is one of the fastest methods to shortcut your way to position #1 without doing too much link building and on-page optimizations

To find these keywords:

Set the Target’s position filter to 11-20

Set Competitors’ positions filter to All competitors in top 10

Check Main positions only


This shows us all the keywords you could be targeting.

Let’s say that we want to write an article about “magento SEO” for our blog.

To do this, look at the keywords’ actual SERP using Keywords Explorer.

Next, open the Competitive Analysis tool and select “keywords”. Then:

Paste the top 3 SERP results in the bottom section

Enter our future article’s slug in the upper section: ahrefs.com/blog/magento-seo

Set all URLs to Exact URL mode since we want to do a page level analysis


Then hit Show keyword opportunities. This shows us all keywords that we could include as potential subtopics in our soon-to-be live article.

“Magento 2 seo” seems to be a recurring keyword variation, along with “magento agency/experts”.
